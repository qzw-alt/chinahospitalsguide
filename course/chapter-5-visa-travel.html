1. Visa Types for Medical Travel

China offers several visa categories relevant to medical tourists. The right choice depends on your nationality, the length of your planned stay, and whether you have a medical invitation from a recognized institution.

Recommended

F Visa (Visit Visa) — for Medical Treatment

The F visa is China's official visa category for non-commercial exchange visits, including medical treatment. It is the most appropriate visa for international patients seeking medical services in China.

  • Duration: Typically single-entry, valid for 3-6 months
  • Stay permitted: Up to 90 days per entry (extendable inside China through the exit-entry bureau)
  • Requirements: Official invitation letter from a Chinese medical institution
  • Processing time: 4-10 business days at most Chinese embassies/consulates
Also Available

L Visa (Tourist Visa)

For short medical visits (under 15 days for most nationalities), a tourist visa (L visa) may be sufficient, particularly if you have a simple outpatient procedure planned.

  • Duration: Single or double entry, valid for 3-6 months
  • Stay permitted: Typically 15-30 days per entry (depending on your passport)
  • Requirements: Hotel booking, itinerary, sometimes invitation letter
  • Limitation: Not ideal for major procedures requiring longer recovery time; extensions in China are complicated

💡 Visa-Free Entry (2026)

As of 2026, China has expanded its visa-free transit programs significantly. Citizens of many countries (including many European, Asian, and American passports) can enter China visa-free for short stays (typically 72-144 hours) via designated ports. However, this does not cover medical treatment — you still need an F or M visa for medical purposes, even if you can enter visa-free for tourism purposes initially.

2. The Visa Invitation Letter

The visa invitation letter (VOI) is the most critical document for obtaining your F visa. It is issued by the Chinese hospital's International Medical Center and is an official document addressed to the Chinese embassy/consulate.

✅ We Coordinate Invitation Letters

When you book through China Hospitals Guide, our coordinators at partner hospitals will issue the official invitation letter on your behalf. You do not need to arrange this separately — it's part of our coordination service. The letter typically takes 3-5 business days to process after you provide your passport details.

What the Invitation Letter Contains

The official visa invitation letter for medical travel typically includes:

  • Your full name, nationality, date of birth, and passport number
  • Hospital name and address
  • Name of treating physician
  • Planned treatment/procedure
  • Planned dates of treatment and estimated length of stay
  • Hospital's official stamp and a contact person
  • Financial responsibility statement (confirming who pays for treatment)

How to Get the Invitation Letter

1
Confirm your hospital appointment

Once your appointment is booked with a partner hospital, the International Medical Center can issue the invitation letter.

2
Submit passport details

Provide: full name (as in passport), nationality, passport number, date of birth, passport issue/expiry dates.

3
Receive the invitation letter

The hospital sends the original invitation letter by courier (3-5 business days). Some hospitals can send PDF copies via email for faster processing — check with your coordinator.

4
Submit visa application with the invitation letter

Include the invitation letter, your passport, completed visa application form, photo, and fee at your local Chinese embassy/consulate.

3. Visa Application Process

Step-by-Step F Visa Application

Step 1: Complete the online visa application form

Go to the Chinese visa application website (cova.mvisaonline.com) and complete Form COVA. Print the confirmation page. Note: unlike the USESTA or Schengen visa, there is no fee calculation — simply complete the form and book an appointment.

Step 2: Gather your documents

Passport (valid 6+ months), photo (48mm x 33mm, white background), visa confirmation page, invitation letter, proof of travel/accommodation.

Step 3: Book an appointment

Book an appointment at your nearest Chinese embassy/consulate visa application center (CVASC). Processing times vary by location — check the specific consulate's website.

Step 4: Submit application and pay fee

Attend your appointment, submit documents, provide biometrics (fingerprints). Pay the visa fee (varies by nationality and processing speed — typically $30-200 USD).

Step 5: Collect your passport with visa

Standard processing: 4-10 business days. Rush processing available at most consulates for additional fee.

⚠ US Citizens: Note Special Requirements

Chinese visa requirements for US citizens have changed recently. US passport holders may face additional documentation requirements. Check with your local Chinese embassy or use a visa service agency for the most current requirements before applying.

4. Travel Planning

Major International Airports in China

Most international patients fly into one of these gateway cities with direct international connections:

  • Beijing Capital International Airport (PEK/PKX) — Best for patients going to Beijing hospitals; largest international hub in China
  • Shanghai Pudong International Airport (PVG) — Best for Shanghai hospitals; major international hub with excellent connections
  • Guangzhou Baiyun International Airport (CAN) — Best for Sun Yat-sen Cancer Center and other Guangdong hospitals
  • Hong Kong International Airport (HKG) — Good alternative with easy connection to Shenzhen hospitals via train/bus; some nationalities benefit from Hong Kong's simpler visa transit rules

Accommodation Near Partner Hospitals

Most major hospitals are located in central urban areas with extensive hotel options nearby. For international patients, we recommend:

  • Serviced apartments — Best for patients staying 1+ weeks; full kitchen, laundry, more space
  • Business hotels — Western-chain hotels (Marriott, Hilton, Hyatt) near hospital districts offer reliable quality and English-speaking staff
  • Boutique hotels — Often more affordable near major hospital areas

Your coordinator can recommend accommodation options near your specific hospital.

Local Transportation in China

  • Taxi/ride-hailing: Use Didi (China's Uber — works with international phone numbers) or regular taxis. Taxis in major cities are very affordable.
  • Metro: Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and other cities have extensive metro systems. Use Google Maps or Amap (高德地图) for navigation.
  • Train: For travel between cities, China's high-speed rail (G/D trains) is fast, comfortable, and affordable. Book via the 12306 app or at station ticket counters.

💡 Payment Apps You Need

China is largely a cashless society. You will need:

  • WeChat Pay or Alipay — Linked to a Chinese bank account for most payments. International versions (WeChat Pay HK, Alipay+) work for some tourists but may not work at hospitals. Ask your coordinator about payment options.
  • International credit cards — Accepted at most international hotels and some hospitals, but not universally.
  • Cash (CNY) — Keep some cash for smaller hospitals and local purchases.

5. Travel Documents Checklist

🧹 Pre-Travel Documents Checklist

✓ Passport — Valid for 6+ months beyond your planned return date, at least 2 blank visa pages
✓ F Visa (in passport) — Obtained from Chinese embassy/consulate with hospital invitation letter
✓ Visa invitation letter — Original or printed copy from the hospital
✓ Medical records in English AND Chinese — Translated and printed; imaging CDs if applicable
✓ Flight itinerary — Confirmation of round-trip flights in and out of China
✓ Hotel confirmation — Booking near the hospital for your entire stay
✓ Travel insurance policy — Including medical evacuation and medical coverage; print the policy document and emergency contact numbers
✓ Emergency contact information — Your coordinator's phone/WeChat, hospital address in Chinese characters
✓ Vaccination certificate — If required by current regulations for your country of origin
✓ Chinese characters card — A card with your hospital's name and address written in Chinese characters to show a taxi driver

📝 Chapter Summary

✅ Key Takeaways

  1. Use the F visa for medical treatment — It allows up to 90 days stay and is the correct visa for medical tourism
  2. The hospital invitation letter is essential — It is your key supporting document for the F visa application
