Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about medical tourism in China

Is medical treatment in China safe?

Yes. Many Chinese hospitals are JCI-accredited (Joint Commission International), meaning they meet the same standards as top US and European hospitals. Look for hospitals with international patient departments and English-speaking staff for the safest experience.

How much can I save by having surgery in China?

Savings typically range from 50-80% compared to Western countries. Common procedures like knee replacements cost £6,000-8,000 in China vs £20,000-25,000 in the UK. Heart surgery can cost $10,000-15,000 vs $100,000-200,000 in the US.

Do I need a visa for medical treatment in China?

Yes, you'll need a visa. Medical tourism visas (M visa) are available for patients seeking treatment in China. Most medical tourism agencies assist with the visa application process and can provide invitation letters from Chinese hospitals.

How do I communicate with my medical team?

International patient departments provide English-speaking coordinators, translators, and often dedicated liaisons who accompany you to appointments. Many hospitals also offer video consultations before you travel.

What happens if there are complications after I return home?

Reputable medical tourism agencies provide ongoing remote follow-up care after your procedure. This includes virtual consultations with your surgeon, coordination with local doctors for any necessary local follow-up, and emergency contact access.

Which hospitals do you recommend?

We partner with over 50 JCI-accredited hospitals across China, including Xiehe Hospital (Beijing), Ruijin Hospital (Shanghai), Fuwai Hospital (Beijing), West China Hospital (Chengdu), and many others. We match patients with the most appropriate facility for their specific condition.

How do I know my medical records will be handled confidentially?

All partner hospitals comply with international patient privacy standards. Your medical records are protected under Chinese healthcare privacy laws and can be transmitted securely through encrypted systems. You're in control of who sees your information.

Can I bring a companion with me?

Absolutely. Most medical tourism packages allow you to bring family members or companions. The companion's travel and accommodation costs are usually separate, but hospitals can recommend nearby accommodation suitable for extended stays.
