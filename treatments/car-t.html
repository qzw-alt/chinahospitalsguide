Why China for CAR-T Therapy?

China has emerged as a global powerhouse in CAR-T cell therapy for three reasons:

  • More products, faster: 7 approved CAR-T therapies as of June 2026 vs 6 in the US — including satri-cel, the world's first CAR-T approved for solid tumors (gastric cancer).
  • 1/5 the cost: Chinese CAR-T therapies are priced $30,000-$80,000 per treatment vs $300,000-$500,000 in the US and $200,000-$400,000 in Singapore. Some hospitals offer compassionate-use pricing even lower.
  • High-volume centers: Chinese hospitals administer more CAR-T treatments annually than most Western centers, giving clinical teams extensive real-world experience managing cytokine release syndrome (CRS) and neurotoxicity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Am I eligible for CAR-T therapy in China?

CAR-T is primarily indicated for certain blood cancers (lymphoma, leukemia, multiple myeloma) and, starting 2026, CLDN18.2-positive gastric cancer. Eligibility depends on your specific diagnosis, prior treatments, and overall health. We help you determine if CAR-T could be an option by connecting your records with treating hospitals.

How long do I need to stay in China for CAR-T treatment?

CAR-T is a multi-week process: approximately 1 week for pre-treatment evaluation and T-cell collection, 2-3 weeks for cell manufacturing, 1 week for infusion and initial monitoring, and 1-2 weeks for recovery observation. Plan for 4-6 weeks total in China.

Is CAR-T in China as effective as in the US?

Clinical outcomes for approved CAR-T products in China are comparable to international results. Chinese CAR-T centers follow the same protocols and monitoring standards as US and European centers. Many products are manufactured in China under GMP standards equivalent to international requirements.

Can I combine CAR-T with other treatments in China?

Yes. Many patients combine CAR-T with other therapies available in China, including targeted therapy, immunotherapy (checkpoint inhibitors), and Traditional Chinese Medicine for supportive care. Your treatment plan is determined by the hospital based on your specific case.
