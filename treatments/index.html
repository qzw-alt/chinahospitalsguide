Understanding the Price Differences

The cost savings in China and other medical tourism destinations are primarily due to lower operating costs, government healthcare subsidies, and different insurance structures — not lower quality of care. Many Chinese hospitals treating international patients have JCI (Joint Commission International) accreditation and use the same equipment and techniques as Western hospitals.

When comparing costs, always factor in: surgeon fees, hospital stay, medication, translation services, and any follow-up care. Our quotes are comprehensive — no hidden fees.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about receiving medical treatment in China.

Is the quality of medical care in China comparable to Western countries?

Yes. Many Chinese hospitals serving international patients have JCI (Joint Commission International) accreditation — the same standard used to evaluate hospitals in the US and Europe. China's top hospitals invest heavily in state-of-the-art equipment, and their specialists often train at leading Western institutions. Due to high procedure volumes, Chinese surgeons frequently have more experience with complex cases than their counterparts in smaller Western hospitals.

How much can I save by getting treatment in China?

Most treatments cost 60-80% less than in the United States, with comparable quality. A heart bypass that costs $100,000+ in the US typically runs $15,000-$40,000 in China. IVF cycles are $5,000-$12,000 versus $20,000-$40,000 in America. These savings apply even after factoring in travel, accommodation, and translation services.

Do doctors and nurses speak English?

The hospitals we partner with have dedicated international patient centers with English-speaking staff. Your assigned patient coordinator provides translation during all medical appointments. Many top surgeons and specialists have international training and publish in English-language journals. You should not experience language barriers during your treatment.

What happens if there are complications after the procedure?

All reputable hospitals we work with have rigorous safety protocols and emergency response systems. If complications arise during your hospital stay, they are addressed immediately at no additional cost. For issues that occur after discharge, we coordinate telemedicine follow-ups with your treating physician at no charge. We also help coordinate with your local doctor for any ongoing care needed at home.

How do I know which hospital is right for my condition?

Our medical team reviews your specific case, medical records, and preferences to recommend the most suitable hospitals and specialists. We consider factors like your diagnosis, treatment type, budget, and location preferences. You receive a detailed comparison of recommended options with projected outcomes, costs, and timelines so you can make an informed decision.

What documents do I need to travel for medical treatment?

Requirements include: a valid passport, visa (we provide invitation letters for medical visas), medical records and诊断 reports translated into English or Chinese, and any relevant imaging (CT, MRI, X-rays). For certain treatments, you may need a letter from your local doctor. We provide a complete checklist based on your specific treatment plan after your initial consultation.

Can I bring a companion or family member?

Absolutely. We encourage companions for most treatments — emotional support aids recovery. Companions receive the same visa invitation letters and can stay in the same or nearby accommodation. For major surgeries, having a companion is strongly recommended. Our coordinators can arrange companion travel packages and provide guidance on local activities for companions during treatment periods.

How long do I need to stay in China for treatment?

Stay duration varies by procedure: minor treatments may require 3-7 days, major surgeries typically require 2-4 weeks including initial recovery in the hospital and nearby accommodation. IVF cycles require 2-3 weeks for the full stimulation and transfer process. Cancer treatments like chemotherapy are scheduled in cycles. We provide detailed timelines with your treatment plan.

Is my medical information kept confidential?

Yes. All hospitals we partner with maintain strict patient confidentiality policies compliant with Chinese healthcare privacy laws. Your medical records, personal information, and treatment details are accessible only to your treating medical team and our coordination staff. We never share your information with third parties without your explicit consent.

What payment methods are accepted?

Most hospitals accept wire transfers, major credit cards (Visa, Mastercard, American Express), and some accept PayPal. Payment is typically made directly to the hospital upon arrival or before procedures. We do not take payments ourselves — all financial transactions are between you and the hospital. We can provide bank transfer guidance and receipt documentation for insurance purposes.

Will my health insurance cover treatment in China?

Most US and European insurance plans do not cover international medical treatment unless it's a life-threatening emergency. However, many international insurers and some employer-sponsored plans are now covering medical tourism procedures. We recommend checking with your insurance provider. For self-pay patients, we can provide detailed medical documentation and receipts for you to submit for reimbursement or tax deduction purposes.

How do I get started?

Simply fill out our contact form or email us your medical situation. You don't need to commit at this stage — an initial consultation is always free. Tell us your diagnosis, what treatment you're considering, and any questions you have. We'll respond within 24 hours with preliminary recommendations and cost estimates. There's no obligation and no pressure — we're here to help you explore all your options.
