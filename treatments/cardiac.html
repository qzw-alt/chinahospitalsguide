Cardiac Procedures We Offer

Our partner hospitals provide the full spectrum of cardiovascular treatments, from diagnostic catheterization to complex robotic surgery.

Coronary Artery Bypass Graft (CABG)

Open Heart Surgery

Also known as heart bypass surgery, CABG creates new pathways for blood to reach the heart by grafting healthy blood vessels to bypass blocked arteries. Our surgeons perform both traditional on-pump and beating-heart (off-pump) bypass surgery.

USA Cost
$75,000-$150,000
China Cost
$15,000-$25,000
Hospital Stay

Recovery
6-12 Weeks
  • Pre-operative cardiac catheterization
  • Anesthesia and surgical team
  • ICU monitoring (48-72 hours)
  • Post-operative medications
  • Follow-up consultation

Heart Valve Replacement / Repair

Specialized Surgery

Surgical treatment for damaged or diseased heart valves. Options include mechanical valves, biological tissue valves, and minimally invasive approaches. TAVR (Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement) available for high-risk patients.

Angioplasty with Stenting

Minimally Invasive

A catheter-based procedure to open narrowed coronary arteries. A small balloon expands the blockage, and a stent (drug-eluting or bare metal) keeps the artery open. Often combined with intravascular imaging (IVUS/OCT).

USA Cost
$30,000-$50,000
China Cost
$5,000-$10,000
Hospital Stay
1-3 Days
Recovery
1-2 Weeks

  • Medication regimen
  • Cardiac rehabilitation plan

Pacemaker & ICD Implantation

Device Therapy

Implantation of electronic devices to regulate heart rhythm. Pacemakers treat slow heart rates (bradycardia), while Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillators (ICDs) monitor and correct dangerous fast rhythms.

Cardiac Catheterization

Diagnostic

Gold-standard diagnostic procedure to evaluate heart function and coronary artery blockages. Includes measurement of pressures, injection of contrast dye, and imaging of heart chambers and vessels.

TAVR / TAVI

Transcatheter

Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement (TAVR) is a minimally invasive alternative to open-heart surgery for aortic valve replacement. A new valve is threaded through a catheter and deployed inside the diseased valve.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about getting cardiac treatment in China.

How much can I save on heart surgery in China compared to the US? +

Most cardiac procedures cost 70-85% less in China than in the United States. For example:

  • Heart bypass surgery (CABG): $75,000-$150,000 (US) vs $15,000-$25,000 (China)
  • Heart valve replacement: $80,000-$200,000 (US) vs $18,000-$35,000 (China)
  • Angioplasty with stent: $30,000-$50,000 (US) vs $5,000-$10,000 (China)

Even after accounting for travel, accommodation, and visa costs, patients typically save $30,000-$100,000 or more.

Are Chinese heart surgeons qualified and experienced? +

Yes. Chinese cardiac surgeons are among the most experienced in the world. Top hospitals like Fuwai perform more heart surgeries annually than almost any hospital globally. Many surgeons have trained at or collaborated with leading Western institutions such as Mayo Clinic, Cleveland Clinic, Harvard, and Stanford. All surgeons in our network are board-certified with extensive subspecialty training.

How do I know which hospital and surgeon are right for my condition? +

Our medical team reviews your records and recommends the most appropriate hospital and surgeon based on your specific condition, medical history, and preferences. We consider factors such as the type of procedure needed, the surgeon's subspecialty expertise, hospital facilities, and your budget. You'll receive a detailed recommendation with all relevant information before making any decisions.

How long does heart surgery take in China? What's the typical hospital stay? +

Hospital stays vary by procedure:

  • Heart bypass (CABG): 7-10 days in hospital, full recovery 6-12 weeks
  • Valve replacement: 6-10 days in hospital, full recovery 4-10 weeks
  • Angioplasty/stent: 1-3 days in hospital, full recovery 1-2 weeks
  • Pacemaker/ICD: 1-3 days in hospital, full recovery 1-2 weeks
  • TAVR: 3-5 days in hospital, full recovery 2-4 weeks

We can arrange extended stay accommodation if needed for recovery before flying home.

Will there be language barriers during my treatment? +

All hospitals in our network have dedicated international patient centers with English-speaking staff. Your patient coordinator will be with you during all medical appointments to translate and ensure clear communication between you and your medical team. Medical records can be provided in English, and consent forms will be explained thoroughly before signing.

What happens when I return home after surgery? +

We provide comprehensive discharge planning including:

  • Detailed summary of your procedure and findings
  • Complete medication list with instructions
  • Dietary and activity guidelines
  • Cardiac rehabilitation program recommendations
  • Follow-up schedule with your local cardiologist
  • Remote consultation availability for questions

We coordinate with your home doctor to ensure seamless ongoing care.

Is it safe to travel for heart surgery? What about flying after surgery? +

Flying after heart surgery is generally safe after the initial recovery period. For most procedures, patients can fly home 7-14 days after surgery. For longer flights, we'll coordinate with your medical team to ensure it's safe and may arrange additional monitoring or-special accommodations. Most patients travel with a companion for added comfort and support during the journey.

What types of heart conditions can be treated in China? +

Our partner hospitals treat the full spectrum of cardiovascular conditions including:

  • Coronary artery disease and blockages
  • Heart valve diseases (aortic, mitral, tricuspid, pulmonary)
  • Heart rhythm disorders (AFib, SVT, bradycardia)
  • Heart failure
  • Congenital heart defects in adults
  • Aortic aneurysms
  • Peripheral vascular disease

Both traditional open surgery and minimally invasive/transcatheter approaches are available.

  2. Receive a consultation from our medical team within 24-48 hours

What if I need a medical evacuation or have complications? +

Complications after cardiac surgery are rare, especially at our vetted top-tier hospitals. However, should any concerns arise:

  • Our hospitals have 24/7 emergency response capabilities
  • We maintain direct communication with your care team
  • Medical evacuation insurance is recommended and can be discussed
  • We provide comprehensive discharge protocols to minimize risks
  • Remote follow-up is available indefinitely for any questions
