Frequently Asked Questions

Is the medical care in China actually good quality?

Yes. Many of China's top hospitals rival or exceed Western standards for specific procedures. China performs more surgeries annually than any other country, giving doctors immense practical experience. Several Chinese hospitals hold JCI accreditation, and many specialists have trained internationally.

How do I know my doctor is qualified?

We provide credentials and backgrounds for all recommended doctors, including their education, training, years of experience, and published research. Many of our recommended doctors have trained at top institutions in the US, UK, or Europe.

What if I don't speak Chinese?

All our partner hospitals have international patient departments with English-speaking staff. We also provide dedicated coordinators for complex cases to ensure smooth communication throughout your treatment.

How do I get a medical visa?

We provide invitation letters and supporting documentation required for your visa application. Many patients apply for a standard tourist visa initially, though a medical visa (M-visa) is also available with our documentation support.

What happens after I return home?

We maintain communication after your treatment and can coordinate follow-up care with local doctors in your home country. We also assist with obtaining medical records and reports for your local healthcare providers.

Is my case too complex for treatment in China?

We handle cases of all complexity levels. For extremely rare conditions, we can facilitate consultations with multiple specialists to determine the best treatment approach. We'll be honest if we believe a case is better managed elsewhere.

Preparing for Your Medical Journey

Successfully traveling to China for medical treatment requires some preparation. Here's what you need to know:

📄 Documentation

You'll need a valid passport (with at least 6 months validity), visa documentation (which we provide), medical records in English, and any relevant imaging or lab results.

💊 Packing Essentials

Bring your regular medications (in original packaging), comfortable clothing suitable for a hospital stay, personal toiletries, and entertainment for downtime during recovery.

💰 Payment Information

Most hospitals accept international credit cards, wire transfers, and sometimes PayPal. You'll typically pay a deposit upon arrival with the balance due before discharge. We provide cost estimates in advance so you can plan accordingly.

⏱️ Timeline Expectations

From initial consultation to treatment completion, the timeline varies by procedure. Simple consultations might require just a few days in China, while major surgery could require 2-4 weeks including recovery time in-country before flying home.

🌡️ Recovery Planning

Plan for adequate recovery time before returning home, especially for major procedures. Your coordinator will advise on recommended in-country recovery periods based on your specific treatment.

📱 Staying Connected

Purchase a local SIM card upon arrival or use international roaming. WeChat is widely used in China for communication. We recommend downloading it before arrival for convenient contact with your coordinator.

The China Medical Travel Experience

Many first-time patients are curious about what it's actually like to travel to China for medical treatment. Here's an overview of the typical experience:

Arrival: Upon landing at a major Chinese airport, your coordinator or driver will meet you with a name sign. You'll be transferred directly to your accommodation or hospital depending on your schedule.

Hospital Admission: International patient floors feature private or semi-private rooms with amenities comparable to hotel accommodations. English-speaking nurses and staff provide care, and your coordinator visits daily.

Treatment: Procedures are performed by experienced specialists using advanced equipment. Chinese surgeons often perform higher volumes of specific procedures than their Western counterparts, contributing to excellent outcomes.

Recovery: Post-procedure care follows international protocols. Hospitals provide detailed recovery instructions, and your coordinator remains available for any questions during the healing process.

Departure: Once cleared by your medical team, we arrange transportation to the airport. We provide complete medical records in English for your home doctor and schedule follow-up consultations as needed.

Follow-Up: Our relationship doesn't end when you board your flight home. We maintain communication for post-treatment questions and coordinate any necessary follow-up care remotely.

Why Medical Tourism to China Makes Sense

China is rapidly becoming one of the world's most popular destinations for medical tourism, and for good reason. Here's why an increasing number of international patients are choosing China for their healthcare needs:

World-Class Healthcare Infrastructure: China has invested heavily in healthcare infrastructure, with state-of-the-art hospitals featuring the latest medical technologies, modern facilities, and internationally trained specialists.

Unmatched Surgical Experience: Chinese surgeons often perform more procedures annually than their counterparts in other countries. Volume correlates with expertise—studies consistently show that surgeons and hospitals performing higher volumes of specific procedures achieve better outcomes.

Cutting-Edge Research and Treatments: China is at the forefront of medical research and offers experimental treatments and clinical trials that may not be available in other countries. From stem cell therapies to advanced cancer treatments, China offers options for patients seeking the latest medical innovations.

Significant Cost Advantages: Even accounting for travel costs, receiving treatment in China typically costs 60-85% less than in Western countries. This makes even major procedures accessible to patients who might otherwise forgo treatment due to cost.

Minimal Wait Times: While patients in countries with nationalized healthcare may wait months for elective procedures, international patients in China can often schedule treatment within days or weeks of deciding to proceed.

Comprehensive Care Packages: Chinese hospitals with international patient departments offer comprehensive packages that include everything from airport pickup to discharge, making the experience seamless for international visitors.
