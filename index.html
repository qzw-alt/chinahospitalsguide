What a first-time visitor needs to understand

The strongest path for a service site like this is not hospital browsing first. It is understanding the offer first, then moving into contact and payment with confidence.

1

Share the case

Tell us the condition, urgency, preferred city, and timeline so we can point you in the right direction.

2

Review the service

See what is included in matching, translation, booking support, and pre-arrival coordination.

3

Choose and pay

Pick the starter or standard package only after you understand scope, refund terms, and likely next actions.

4

Begin coordination

After payment, you send records, we confirm priorities, and your case moves into active handling.

Choose the right starting point

Different visitors need different entry points. The website should make those paths obvious instead of pushing everyone into the same content pile.

I am still exploring

Best for patients who want guidance before spending money.

  • Use the free case review
  • Get pointed to the right package
  • Ideal if you are still comparing cities or specialties

I already know I want support

Best for patients ready to buy a coordination package now.

  • Go directly to pricing
  • Pay securely with PayPal
  • See refund terms before purchase

I already paid or need a person

Best for patients who need direct contact or follow-up.

  • Use the contact hub
  • Choose email or WhatsApp
  • See what to prepare next

What makes the offer believable

For a medical travel service, trust comes from clarity. Patients want to see that the process is manageable and the service boundaries are honest.

Independent role explained clearly

We coordinate and guide. The hospital delivers treatment. That line needs to stay visible so patients know what they are paying for.

Patient stories and process

Stories help, but they work best when paired with a practical explanation of the next step a new patient should take.

Post-payment expectations

The site should tell patients how fast you reply, what documents to prepare, and how coordination begins after payment.

Hospitals still matter, but after the offer is understood

Browsing hospitals supports conversion. It should not replace the main service journey.
