The dental tourism market in China is projected to grow significantly through the decade, driven partly by international patients looking beyond their home systems. If you have been quoted thousands for an implant back home, it is worth knowing what China actually costs right now, and what you are getting for that price.

This guide covers typical price ranges for common implant procedures, which hospital and clinic types handle international patients, and what the overall process looks like from first consultation to final crown.

Why Dental Implants in China? The Short Version

The headline reason is price. A single implant that runs $3,000 to $5,000 at a private practice in the United States typically costs $800 to $1,500 in China, using the same or equivalent implant systems. For full-arch work like All-on-4, the gap is even starker: $8,000 to $15,000 in China versus $25,000 to $50,000 in the US.

Beyond cost, the main practical advantages are:

  • Clinics serving international patients use the same brands found in Western dental offices: Straumann, Nobel Biocare, Astra Tech, Ankylos, Zimmer, and others
  • Many implant specialists have trained abroad and see international patients as a routine part of their practice
  • Advanced imaging and guided surgery are standard at mid-tier and above clinics
  • Wait times are short; you can usually get an appointment within one to two weeks
  • China is a major dental manufacturing hub, so the supply chain for premium implant systems is well established

What Affects the Price in China?

A few factors will move the number up or down from the ranges above:

  • Implant brand: Straumann and Nobel Biocare cost more than mid-range options. Korean brands like Dentium and Osstem offer a middle price point without sacrificing quality.
  • Clinic tier: International arms of university hospitals, JCI-accredited private hospitals, and dedicated dental tourism clinics all price differently.
  • Bone condition: If you need bone grafting or a sinus lift, that adds to the total.
  • Material for the crown: Zirconia and E-max cost more than standard porcelain-fused-to-metal.
  • Number of implants: Multi-implant cases get volume discounts at some clinics, though the per-unit price is what matters most for most patients.

Types of Dental Implants Available

1. Single Dental Implant

One implant post to replace a single missing tooth. Includes the implant, abutment, and a crown. This is the most straightforward procedure and the one most first-time dental tourists start with.

2. All-on-4

Four implants per arch support a full fixed bridge. Designed for patients missing all or most teeth in one jaw. You leave the clinic with temporary teeth the same day or within a few days of surgery.

3. All-on-6

Six implants per arch. The extra two implants provide more stability and are useful when bone density is moderate rather than severe. Not everyone is a candidate for All-on-4, which is why assessment matters.

4. Zygomatic Implants

For patients with significant bone loss who would otherwise need major bone grafting. These longer implants anchor into the cheekbone rather than the jaw. The procedure is more complex and not available at every clinic.

5. Mini Dental Implants

Smaller-diameter implants used for narrow ridges or as temporary anchors. They are less invasive but also less load-bearing than standard implants. Some patients use them for overdentures.

Implant Brands You Will Find in China

China has a large domestic manufacturing sector for dental implants, but most international-focused clinics use imported systems. Here is what is commonly available:

  • Premium: Nobel Biocare (Switzerland), Straumann (Switzerland), Astra Tech (Sweden)
  • Mid-Range: Ankylos (Germany), Zimmer (USA), Biomet 3i (USA)
  • Value: Dentium (Korea), Osstem (Korea), MIS (Israel)
  • Other systems: CAMLOG, BEGO, and others depending on the clinic

If you already have a brand preference from your home dentist, it is worth confirming availability before booking. The major brands are widely stocked, but specific product lines may vary.

What Your Treatment Timeline Looks Like

Short Stay: 3 to 5 Days

Workable for a single implant when bone quality is good and no extractions are needed:

  1. Day 1: Arrive, consultation, 3D CT scan, confirm treatment plan
  2. Day 2-3: Implant placement surgery (typically 1-2 hours under local anesthetic or sedation)
  3. Day 4-5: Follow-up check, receive temporary crown or healing abutment, fly home

The permanent crown requires 3 to 6 months of healing first. You can either return to China for it, or have your local dentist complete the final restoration using the records and abutment specs provided by the Chinese clinic.

Longer Stay: 10 to 14 Days

Makes sense if you need extractions, bone grafting, or want the permanent crown placed before leaving:

  1. Day 1-2: Consultation, CT scan, any extractions
  2. Day 3-5: Healing period, implant placement
  3. Day 6-10: Abutment placement, impressions for crown
  4. Day 11-14: Crown fitting and final adjustments

All-on-4 or All-on-6: 7 to 10 Days

You will leave with a temporary fixed prosthesis. The permanent one goes in after the 3-to-6-month healing period, either on a return trip or with your local dentist.

Common Questions

How long do dental implants last?

A well-placed implant with good oral hygiene can last 20 years or more. Success rates at experienced Chinese clinics are in the 95-98% range, which is in line with what you would expect in the US or Australia. The crown itself may need replacing after 10-15 years depending on material and wear.

Is the surgery painful?

Most patients report minimal discomfort. The surgery is done under local anesthetic or light sedation. Soreness afterward is manageable with standard over-the-counter pain relievers for most people.

How long do I need to stay in China?

Plan for a minimum of 3 to 5 days for a single implant. All-on-4 cases typically require 7 to 10 days for the initial phase. The permanent crown is a separate step 3 to 6 months later, either back in China or with your home dentist.

Will anyone speak English?

At the clinics most international patients use, yes. English-speaking coordinators and dentists are standard for the patient-facing side of the practice. The clinical team may have limited English, which is why the coordinator matters. We specifically match patients with clinics that have established international patient workflows.

What exactly is included in the quoted price?

For the typical per-implant quote, you can expect: initial consultation, 3D CT scan, the implant post itself, the abutment, and a temporary crown. The permanent crown may or may not be included depending on the package. Bone grafting, extractions, and sinus lifts are usually listed separately. Always get a line-item breakdown before committing.

Can I combine treatment with travel?

Yes, and a lot of patients do. Most clinics in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen are centrally located and easy to combine with city sightseeing between appointments. We can also help arrange accommodation and airport transfers through partner services.
