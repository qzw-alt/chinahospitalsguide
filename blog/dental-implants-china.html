Dental Implant Cost in China (2026)

Compare implant costs across China, US, Thailand, and Mexico — with named hospital prices, All-on-4/All-on-6 breakdowns, patient case, expert data, and risk guidance.

Updated March 2026 10 min read For international patients comparing treatment costs

Dental implants in China are increasingly considered by international patients who want lower treatment costs without giving up access to major cities, private clinics, or internationally known implant systems. The key question is not only price — it is whether the clinic, implant brand, and treatment plan fit your case and travel timeline.

Quick cost snapshot

From $800 per implant in China
Often 60 to 80% lower than typical US private pricing

Why patients compare dental implant treatment in China

  • Lower overall cost: often much lower than private pricing in the US, UK, or Australia.
  • Wide implant brand choice: premium and mid-range systems are commonly available.
  • Faster scheduling: many clinics can see international patients sooner than overloaded local systems.
  • Digital planning: CT scans, guided surgery, and restorative planning are common in higher-end clinics.
  • Large city options: Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen all offer different clinic styles and price levels.

All-on-4 and All-on-6 costs in China (detailed breakdown)

Full-arch solutions like All-on-4 and All-on-6 are among the most common treatment plans for patients with multiple missing teeth. China offers significant savings compared to the US, with pricing varying by clinic tier and materials.

All-on-4 cost range — China (per arch)

Economy clinic tier $8,000–$10,000
Mid-range private clinic $10,000–$13,000
Premium international hospital $13,000–$15,000

What's typically included: 4 implants, titanium framework, acrylic or composite temporary prosthesis, abutments, surgery, and follow-up. Final zirconia prosthesis usually quoted separately ($2,000–$4,000 additional per arch).

All-on-6 cost range — China (per arch)

Economy clinic tier $12,000–$14,000
Mid-range private clinic $14,000–$16,000
Premium international hospital $16,000–$18,000

What's typically included: 6 implants, reinforced titanium framework, acrylic or composite provisional prosthesis, abutments, surgery, and follow-up. Final layered zirconia prosthesis typically $2,500–$5,000 additional per arch.

Key All-on-4 / All-on-6 cost factors

  • Temporary vs final prosthesis: Provisional is included in most quotes; final zirconia or layered restorations are usually extra
  • Implant brand: Nobel Biocare and Straumann command higher prices than value-tier systems
  • Bone quality and grafting needs: Cases requiring sinus lift or bone graft add $500–$2,000+
  • Sedation type: Local anesthesia is standard; IV sedation adds $300–$800
  • Upper vs lower arch: Both arches treated simultaneously may reduce per-arch costs at some clinics

Common dental implant treatment types in China

1. Single dental implant

Used when one missing tooth needs replacement. Usually includes the implant post, abutment, and crown, though some clinics quote these items separately. Recovery is typically 3–6 months before final crown placement.

2. Multiple implants (3–4 units)

Adjacent or scattered missing teeth replaced with individual implants. Each site is evaluated independently for bone quality and spacing.

3. All-on-4 dental implants

Full-arch restoration using four implants. Often chosen by patients with multiple missing teeth who want a lower implant count and faster provisional restoration. Temporary prosthesis can often be fitted within days of implant placement.

4. All-on-6 dental implants

Full-arch restoration using six implants. Provides added stability compared to All-on-4 and may suit patients with different bone or bite considerations. Better load distribution can extend prosthetic lifespan.

5. Zygomatic implants

Used in selected complex cases with severe upper-jaw bone loss. These anchor into the cheekbone rather than the maxilla. More specialized and requires careful pre-operative planning and experienced surgical teams.

6. Mini dental implants

Sometimes used in narrower ridges, temporary restorations, or specific clinical situations. They are not simply a cheaper version of standard implants and should be matched to the case by a qualified clinician.

Implant brands commonly available in China

Many clinics in China offer internationally recognized systems alongside domestic options. Brand choice affects pricing, prosthetic compatibility, and long-term restoration options.

  • Premium systems: Nobel Biocare (Sweden), Straumann (Switzerland), Astra Tech (Sweden/Denmark)
  • Mid-range systems: Ankylos (Germany), Zimmer (USA), Biomet 3i (USA)
  • Value-focused systems: Dentium (Korea), Osstem (Korea), MIS (Israel)
  • Domestic Chinese systems: Cicon (Zhejiang), WeClean (Shanghai), and others with growing market presence
  • Other systems: CAMLOG (Germany), BEGO (Germany), and others depending on clinic inventory and surgeon preference

Dental implant survival rates

According to a 2024 systematic review published in the International Journal of Implant Dentistry, implant survival rates at 10 years for premium systems (Straumann, Nobel Biocare) average 94–96% in controlled patient populations. Standard-tier systems show slightly wider variance (88–94%) depending on study methodology and patient selection criteria.

China dental tourism volume

Dental tourism to China is a growing segment within medical travel. Major receiving cities include Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Shenzhen. Patients primarily come from the US, Australia, the UK, Canada, and Southeast Asia. Clinics serving international patients typically offer English-language coordination, digital treatment planning, and itemized international quotes.

Key expert recommendations for treatment planning

  • Get at least two treatment plans before committing — ideally from different clinic tiers (one private clinic, one hospital dental department)
  • Request specific implant brand and model in writing, not just "premium implant"
  • Ask for itemized quotes: consultation, imaging, implant, abutment, crown/temporary, medications, follow-up
  • Confirm prosthetic stages: temporary prosthesis vs. final restoration and their respective costs
  • Plan for a staged return visit for final restoration on full-arch cases — most clinics do not rush osseointegration timelines

Risks and important considerations

Like any surgical procedure, dental implant treatment carries risks. Being informed about them is part of making a good decision.

⚠ Surgical risks

Infection, nerve injury (particularly in lower jaw implants), sinus perforation (upper jaw), and implant failure. These risks exist in every country and are managed through proper case selection, surgical skill, and post-operative care.

⚠ Implant brand consistency

Ensure your implant brand and model are documented in writing. Some clinics use multiple systems. If you need future maintenance or prosthetic work in another country, knowing the exact brand and connection type is essential.

⚠ Prosthetic quality variation

Not all crowns and bridges are equal. Materials (zirconia vs. composite vs. acrylic), manufacturing method (in-house lab vs. outsourced), and technician skill all affect long-term performance and aesthetics.

⚠ Follow-up and aftercare challenges

If complications arise after you return home, coordinating care across countries can be difficult and costly. Confirm your home dentist is willing to provide follow-up care before you travel for treatment.

⚠ Language and communication gaps

Even in English-speaking clinics, miscommunication about treatment expectations can occur. Request written treatment plans, ask for photo documentation of your case, and confirm all numbers in writing before proceeding.

⚠ Staged treatment realities

Full-arch cases (All-on-4, All-on-6) typically require a return visit 3–6 months after implant placement. Patients who assume one trip is sufficient may face unexpected travel costs and scheduling conflicts.

Your dental implant timeline in China

Option 1: First-visit placement and temporary restoration

  1. Day 1: consultation, imaging, treatment planning
  2. Day 2-3: implant surgery
  3. Day 4-5: follow-up and temporary restoration where appropriate

Permanent crowns often require healing time and may be completed later, either on a return visit or through a coordinated plan.

Option 2: Longer stay for more treatment steps

  1. Day 1-2: consultation, CT scan, extractions if needed
  2. Day 3-5: implant placement and early healing
  3. Day 6-10: abutment work or impressions depending on the case
  4. Day 11-14: possible restorative steps for selected treatment plans

Option 3: All-on-4 or All-on-6 planning

Many patients receive a temporary prosthesis first and return later for the final prosthetic stage. This is why realistic travel planning matters as much as the headline price.

Frequently asked questions

How long do dental implants usually last?

With good maintenance and a suitable treatment plan, implants can last many years — often 15–25+ years. What matters most is not the country alone but bone quality, bite forces, oral hygiene, prosthetic design, and ongoing follow-up.

Is implant surgery painful?

The procedure is typically done with local anesthesia, sometimes with sedation depending on the clinic and case. Most patients describe the recovery period as more relevant than the procedure itself.

How long do I need to stay in China?

That depends on whether you are doing consultation only, placement plus a temporary restoration, or a more complex full-arch plan. Many patients still need a staged approach rather than one single trip.

Do dentists in China speak English?

Some international hospitals and private clinics offer English-speaking staff or coordinators. It is still worth confirming the exact communication setup before you book.

What is usually included in the implant price?

It varies. Some quotes include consultation, imaging, implant, abutment, crown, and medications, while others split those items. Comparing like for like is essential.

Can I combine treatment with tourism?

Yes, but only if the treatment schedule, healing expectations, and follow-up needs allow it. Travel plans should support the treatment timeline, not fight it.

How do All-on-4 costs in China compare to Thailand and Mexico?

All-on-4 in China typically ranges $8,000–$15,000 per arch, compared to $10,000–$18,000 in Thailand and $8,000–$15,000 in Mexico. China and Mexico offer the most competitive entry-level pricing; Thailand sits in the mid-range. Premium international clinics in all countries narrow the gap.

What should I ask before booking a clinic in China?

  • Exact implant brand and model ( Straumann, Nobel, etc.)
  • Itemized cost breakdown
  • Prosthetic materials and lab used
  • Number of years the surgeon has placed implants
  • What happens if there is a complication after I return home
  • Whether my home dentist can provide follow-up maintenance
