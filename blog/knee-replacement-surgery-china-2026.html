Why implants cost less in China

China's top hospitals purchase implants directly from manufacturers at wholesale prices. In the US, the same implant might be marked up 200-400% by the hospital and distributor before reaching the patient. Chinese hospitals pass these savings onto international patients.

Same brands, same quality: Zimmer Biomet, Stryker, DePuy Synthes, and Smith & Nephew all have FDA approval and CE marking. The implants in a Chinese hospital are identical to those used in Western hospitals.

Section 5: Patient Journey — From First Consultation to Full Recovery

Most international patients complete their knee replacement journey in 18-24 days. Here's a day-by-day breakdown of what to expect:

Phase 1: Pre-Travel (Weeks 2-4 Before Departure)

Day -21 to -14: Send your medical records, X-rays, and MRI scans to your chosen hospital for review. Include any relevant blood work and a letter from your current physician.

Day -14 to -10: Receive your personalized treatment plan, cost estimate, and surgeon bio. Schedule a video consultation with your assigned international coordinator.

Day -10 to -7: Apply for medical visa (M visa). The hospital provides an official invitation letter. Book flights and arrange airport transfer with the hospital's patient services.

Day -7 to -1: Prepare questions for your surgeon, arrange post-return care with your local doctor, and purchase travel insurance that covers medical complications abroad.

Phase 2: Arrival and Pre-Op (Days 1-2)

  1. Day 1: Land at Beijing/Shanghai/Guangzhou airport. Hospital driver meets you with a name sign. Transfer to partner hotel or hospital guest room.
  2. Day 1 evening: Rest and adjust to time zone. Light meals recommended.
  3. Day 2 morning: Comprehensive pre-operative assessment at the hospital:
    • Blood tests (CBC, coagulation, infectious disease screening)
    • ECG and chest X-ray
    • Final knee imaging and surgical planning
  4. Day 2 afternoon: Meet your surgeon for final consultation. Review implant options (standard vs. high-flexibility). Sign consent forms.

Phase 3: Surgery and Hospital Stay (Days 3-9)

Day 3 (Surgery Day):

  • Nothing by mouth after midnight
  • Pre-operative medications (antibiotic prophylaxis)
  • Surgery duration: 1-2 hours for single knee, 2.5-4 hours for bilateral
  • Recovery room observation: 2-4 hours
  • Transfer to private/semi-private room

Days 4-5 (Early Recovery):

  • Pain management with IV medications transitioning to oral
  • Physical therapist visits twice daily
  • First assisted standing: Day 4
  • First steps with walker: Day 4-5
  • wound drainage removal

Days 6-7 (Mobility Progress):

  • Transition from walker to cane
  • Climbing stairs with assistance
  • Wound inspection and dressing change
  • Discharge planning with rehab instructions

Phase 4: Outpatient Recovery (Days 10-18)

After hospital discharge, most patients stay at a nearby hotel and return to the hospital daily for outpatient physiotherapy:

  1. Days 10-12: Intensive physiotherapy sessions (2 hours daily). Focus on range of motion exercises and gentle strengthening.
  2. Days 13-14: Continued rehab. Stitch/suture removal typically on Day 12-14.
  3. Days 15-18: Advanced walking exercises, balance training, and discharge readiness assessment.

Phase 5: Final Clearance and Return (Days 18-24)

Day 18-20: Final orthopedic evaluation. X-rays confirm implant positioning. Surgeon provides written clearance to fly.

Day 20-22: Travel home. Most patients fly economy class with aisle seats for easier movement. Compression stockings and mobility aids recommended.

Week 4-6 home: Continue prescribed home exercises. Follow-up with local orthopedic surgeon per the discharge records provided.

Section 8: Risks and Mitigation — Being Realistic About Complications

No surgery is without risk. Here's an honest assessment of what can go wrong and how top Chinese hospitals minimize these risks:

Clinical Risks

Risk 1: Infection

Incidence: 0.5-1.2% at top Chinese hospitals (vs. 1-2% global average)

Mitigation:

  • Pre-operative antibiotics administered 30-60 minutes before incision
  • Ultra-clean operating theaters with positive pressure ventilation
  • Antibiotic-impregnated cement used for implant fixation
  • Early mobilization protocols reduce post-op infection risk
  • 24/7 infection monitoring during hospital stay

What to do if it happens: Treatment typically involves 6 weeks of IV antibiotics. Most infections are successfully treated without implant removal if caught early.

Risk 2: Blood Clots (DVT/PE)

Incidence: 0.5-1% at top hospitals with proper prophylaxis

Mitigation:

  • Routine anticoagulation medications for 2-3 weeks post-op
  • Compression stockings and sequential compression devices
  • Early mobilization (walking by Day 1-2)
  • Hydration protocols
  • Pre-flight assessment includes D-dimer testing

Risk 3: Implant Loosening or Failure

Incidence: 1-2% at 10 years (premium implants)

Mitigation:

  • Use only FDA/CE approved implants from established manufacturers
  • Computer-navigated or robotic-assisted placement for precision
  • Appropriate implant selection for patient's age, weight, activity level
  • Patient education on activity restrictions during bone ingrowth period

Risk 4: Stiffness or Limited Range of Motion

Incidence: 5-8% of patients require manipulation under anesthesia

Mitigation:

  • Aggressive physiotherapy beginning Day 1 post-op
  • Continuous passive motion (CPM) machines provided
  • Patient compliance emphasized during rehab period
  • Regular range-of-motion assessments during follow-up

Non-Clinical Risks

Risk 5: Language Barriers

Mitigation:

  • All recommended hospitals have dedicated international departments
  • English-speaking nurses assigned to international patient floors
  • 24/7 coordinator available by phone/WeChat/WhatsApp
  • All consent forms and discharge instructions provided in English

Risk 6: Continuity of Care After Returning Home

Mitigation:

  • Comprehensive discharge summary provided in English
  • All imaging and operative reports given to patient
  • Pre-arranged follow-up appointments with local orthopedic surgeon
  • Hospital remains available for remote consultation via email/video
  • Digital copies of all records accessible via patient portal

Risk 7: Post-Discharge Complications While Flying

Mitigation:

  • Surgeon must clear patient for air travel (typically Day 14+)
  • Compression stockings mandatory during flight
  • aisle seats recommended for mobility
  • Short layovers suggested to reduce sitting time
  • Travel insurance with medical evacuation coverage recommended

Red Flags — When to Choose a Different Option

Consider alternatives to surgery in China if:

  • You require highly specialized revision surgery from a specific surgeon
  • You have complex comorbidities requiring multi-specialty management
  • You need immediate surgery (within days)
  • You have severe mobility limitations preventing air travel
  • Your home country's surgeon recommends against traveling

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do I need to stay in China?

Plan for 2-3 weeks. Surgery on day 2-3, hospital stay 5-7 days, then 7-10 days of outpatient physiotherapy and follow-ups before flying home.

Will my insurance cover it?

Some international insurers cover overseas treatment. Check with your provider. Many patients use the savings to pay out-of-pocket and still come out ahead.

What about the implants? Are they safe?

Top hospitals use FDA-approved implants from Zimmer, Stryker, DePuy - the same brands used in Western hospitals. You can request specific brands.

Can I get both knees done at once?

Yes, bilateral knee replacement is common. Recovery is slightly longer, but you only make one trip. Most patients choose this option.

What if something goes wrong after I return home?

Complications are rare, but hospitals provide 24/7 remote consultation. Your local orthopedic surgeon can handle most issues using the detailed discharge records provided.

How do I verify surgeon credentials?

Ask the hospital for your surgeon's CV, including international training, number of procedures performed, and publication history. Top hospitals are transparent about surgeon qualifications.

What's not included in the quoted price?

Typically not included: flights, accommodation, meals, travel insurance, and any treatment for pre-existing conditions discovered during pre-op workup.
