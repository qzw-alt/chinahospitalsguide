Breast Augmentation in China 2026: Cost, Top Hospitals & Complete Patient Guide

Updated May 2026 · Written by China Hospitals Guide · 12 min read

Breast augmentation in China costs ¥20,000–60,000 ($2,800–$8,300) — roughly half to one-third of what you'd pay in the United States, Australia, or South Korea. What Drives the Price Difference Between China and the US?

The cost gap isn't a quality signal — it's structural. Here's the honest breakdown:

  • Insurance overhead: American clinics employ 3–5 full-time staff just to manage insurance billing. Chinese hospitals don't have this cost.
  • Medical litigation: Malpractice insurance premiums in the US can run $30,000–$100,000 per year for a plastic surgeon. Chinese hospitals carry far lower premiums, which reflects in procedure pricing.
  • Facility fees: American surgical centers charge facility fees of $1,500–$4,500 on top of surgeon fees. Chinese hospital stays — including overnight recovery — are billed at government-guided rates.
  • Implant pricing: US patients pay brand-name premiums on implants. Chinese hospital procurement contracts through NMPA-approved suppliers keep implant costs lower.
  • Volume: Major Chinese plastic surgery centers perform 50–200 breast procedures per month, spreading overhead across more cases.

Top Hospitals for Breast Augmentation in China

These are the hospitals most frequently recommended by international patient coordinators and medical tourism agencies. All handle foreign patients regularly and have English-speaking departments.

Shanghai Ninth People's Hospital (上海第九人民医院)

Affiliated with Shanghai Jiao Tong University School of Medicine, this is one of China's most respected plastic and reconstructive surgery centers. The hospital's Breast Surgery Department handles both reconstructive and aesthetic cases. International patients are assigned a case coordinator who manages appointments and translation. As a public teaching hospital, prices here are at the lower end of the range but the surgical quality matches any private clinic in the country.

Shanghai Shengya Medical Plastic Surgery Hospital

A private hospital specializing in aesthetic procedures. Known for the Three-Plane High-SMAS Facelift technique and breast procedures including Motiva implant placement. Dr. Wang Jingang leads the Facial Rejuvenation Department and is recognized for natural-looking results. The hospital has a dedicated international patient floor with English-speaking staff.

Beijing Fengmao Medical Beauty

One of Beijing's most established private aesthetic hospitals, Fengmao serves a significant international patient base. The hospital offers a range of breast procedures from conservative augmentation to combination lifts. Pricing is transparent and quoted in advance with no hidden charges.

Motiva Ergonomix Premium ergonomic implant that adapts to body position; higher fill ratio for natural movement ¥15,000–25,000 additional

⚠️ Verify NMPA Approval: Only NMPA-approved implants are legal for use in licensed Chinese hospitals. Ask your hospital to confirm the implant brand and approval number. Do not accept "custom" or "grey market" implants offered at suspiciously low prices — these are illegal and unsafe.

The Procedure: What to Expect Step by Step

Step 1 — Initial Consultation (Virtual, Before You Travel)

Most hospitals offer free virtual consultations via WeChat, WhatsApp, or email. Send photos (front and side views) and describe your goals. The surgeon will recommend implant size and type. Get the recommendation in writing, including the specific implant model and cc volume.

Step 2 — Travel and Arrival

Fly into Shanghai (PVG/Hongqiao) or Beijing (PEK). Most hospitals offer airport pickup for international patients. You'll need a valid passport and, depending on your nationality, a visa. China offers visa-free transit for many nationalities (up to 144 hours).

Step 3 — In-Person Consultation and Pre-Op Tests

At the hospital, you'll have a physical examination including breast ultrasound and standard bloodwork. The surgeon confirms the surgical plan. You may try on sizers (temporary implant shells worn in a bra) to feel the difference in size.

Step 4 — Surgery

Surgery takes 1.5–3 hours under general anesthesia. Common incision approaches:

  • Inframammary fold (under the breast crease) — most common, best access
  • Peri-areolar (around the nipple) — less visible scar but higher risk of sensation changes
  • Transaxillary (through the armpit) — scar-free on breast but technically demanding

Implants are placed either submuscular (under the pectoral muscle) or subfascial (under the fascia but over the muscle). Your surgeon will recommend based on your anatomy and tissue quality.

Step 5 — Hospital Recovery

Most patients stay 1–3 nights in the hospital. Drains (small tubes to collect fluid) are typically removed on day 3–5. Compression garments are worn for 4–6 weeks post-op.

Step 6 — Follow-Up and Travel Home

Sutures are removed around day 7–10. Most international patients fly home by day 10–14 after receiving clearance from the surgeon. Remote photo follow-up is arranged for 1 month, 3 months, and 6 months post-op.

How to Choose a Surgeon in China

Not all plastic surgeons in China have the same training or experience. Here's what to verify:

  • Board certification: Look for surgeons certified by the Chinese Association of Plastic Surgery or who trained at a Three-A hospital (China's top tier). Public teaching hospitals affiliated with medical universities are generally the most rigorously vetted.
  • Volume: Ask how many breast procedures the surgeon performs per month. Top surgeons at major centers do 30–80 breast cases monthly.
  • Before/after photos: Request a portfolio of at least 20 cases matching your body type and goals. Be skeptical of surgeons who only show "perfect" cases or won't share photos.
  • Hospital accreditation: JCI (Joint Commission International) accreditation is the global gold standard. A growing number of Chinese hospitals hold JCI certification.
  • Language support: Confirm the hospital has English-speaking coordinators who will be present during consultations and post-op visits — not just in marketing materials.
💡 Pro Tip: WeChat is the standard communication tool for medical consultations in China. Before your trip, download WeChat and connect with your hospital's patient coordinator. This also lets you send follow-up photos after you've returned home.

Breast Augmentation vs Alternatives

If you're exploring options, here's how breast augmentation in China compares to alternatives:

  • Breast lift (mastopexy): Raises and firms existing breast tissue without implants. Ideal for patients with significant ptosis (sagging). Can be combined with augmentation. Cost in China: ¥25,000–45,000.
  • Fat transfer breast augmentation: Uses your own fat instead of implants. More natural feel but limited to 0.5–1 cup size increase per session. Requires two sessions typically. Best for patients seeking modest enhancement.
  • Non-surgical options: Vacuum bras, hormone creams, and "breast enhancement" supplements have no clinically proven efficacy. Do not waste money on these.
  • Korea vs China: South Korea performs more breast procedures per capita and has a highly developed aesthetic surgery culture. However, prices are higher ($5,000–10,000 for augmentation in Seoul) and waiting times longer. China offers comparable surgical outcomes at significantly lower prices.
