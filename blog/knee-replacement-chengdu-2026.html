Total Knee Replacement (TKR) Cost Breakdown: What's Included in Chengdu?

Unlike Western hospitals, Chengdu medical centers typically offer bundled packages. Here's what's included in the typical Total Knee Replacement (TKR) cost in Chengdu:

  • Pre-operative workup: Lab tests, imaging, anesthesia consultation, specialist consultation - included
  • Surgical procedure: Surgeon fees, anesthesia, operating room, implant/IOL (if applicable), sterile supplies - included
  • Hospital stay: 21-28 days in private or semi-private room, all medications, nursing care, meals - included
  • Post-op care: Discharge medications, follow-up appointments, dressing changes, rehabilitation (if needed) - typically included for 1 year
  • International services: Airport pickup, translation, visa invitation letter, English coordinator - typically included at top hospitals
  • NOT typically included: International flights ($800-2000 round trip), accommodation for companion ($50-100/night), travel insurance, optional sightseeing

💡 Money-saving tip: Many Chengdu hospitals offer 10-20% discounts for cash payment or for booking multiple procedures. Ask your international coordinator about package deals.

How to Book Total Knee Replacement (TKR) in Chengdu: Step-by-Step

  1. Submit your case (free): Send recent medical records, imaging, and any prior treatment history to your chosen Chengdu hospital's international department. Most respond within 2-3 business days with treatment plan and quote.
  2. Online consultation: Schedule a video call with the surgeon (English available at top hospitals). Discuss goals, risks, expected outcomes, and finalize surgical plan.

  5. Pre-op appointment (Day 1-2): Comprehensive examination, bloodwork, imaging review, final surgical plan confirmation, anesthesia consultation.
  6. Surgery day: Procedure typically takes 1-4 hours. Total Knee Replacement (TKR) usually 1-2 hours. Same-day or next-day discharge for many procedures.
  7. Recovery in Chengdu (Days 3-7-28): Daily wound checks, medication management, gradual return to normal activities. Outpatient rehabilitation if needed.
  8. Final clearance & departure (Day 28): Surgeon clearance, discharge summary, prescription medications, follow-up schedule. Fit-to-fly letter provided.
  9. Remote follow-up: Hospital international department stays in touch via WeChat/email for 6-12 months. Local follow-up with your home physician encouraged.

Recovery Timeline: What to Expect

Recovery varies by procedure and individual. Here's a general timeline for Total Knee Replacement (TKR) recovery:

  • Days 1-3: Rest, pain management, light activity only. Most patients mobile with assistance.
  • Days 4-7: Gradual return to light activities. Stitches removal (if non-dissolvable).
  • Days 7-14: Significant improvement. Most patients cleared for light work and travel home.
  • Weeks 2-4: Resume most normal activities. Avoid strenuous exercise or heavy lifting.
  • Weeks 4-12: Continued improvement. Final results becoming visible. Full clearance for sports/strenuous activities.
  • 3-6 months: Final results, full healing, scar maturation.

Risks and Safety Considerations

All surgical procedures carry risks. For Total Knee Replacement (TKR) in Chengdu, top hospitals report:

  • Common (1-5%): Bruising, swelling, mild discomfort, temporary numbness - resolve within weeks
  • Less common (0.1-1%): Infection, bleeding requiring intervention, asymmetry, scarring - managed with medication or minor procedures
  • Rare (<0.1%): Serious complications requiring revision surgery, nerve damage, blood clots - choose high-volume centers to minimize

How to minimize risks: Choose JCI-accredited or top-tier 3A hospitals, verify surgeon credentials (board certification, 200+ case volume), follow all pre/post-op instructions, attend all follow-up appointments, and disclose all medical history including medications and supplements.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How much does knee replacement cost in Chengdu?

Total knee replacement (one knee): $8,500-$13,000. Bilateral (both knees same surgery): $15,000-$22,000. Robotic MAKO knee replacement: $11,000-$15,000. Includes pre-op imaging, surgery, 5-7 nights hospital, implant, medications, in-patient rehab, 6-month follow-up. Compare to US: $40,000-$60,000 per knee (often NOT including implant or rehab).

2. Is robotic MAKO knee replacement worth the extra cost in Chengdu?

**Yes, for most patients.** MAKO robotic-assisted knee replacement offers: 1) More precise implant positioning (2-3x more accurate than manual), 2) Better soft tissue balance, 3) Faster recovery (often walking day 1 vs day 2-3), 4) Lower revision rate long-term. The $2,000-$3,000 premium is well worth it for active patients. West China Hospital surgeons perform 1,000+ MAKO cases per year.

3. How long is recovery after knee replacement in Chengdu?

Plan 21-28 days in Chengdu. **Days 1-3:** Surgery + early mobilization (walking with walker Day 1). **Days 4-7:** Inpatient rehab (PT 2x/day). **Days 7-14:** Outpatient rehab (PT 1-2x/day). **Days 14-21:** Strengthening, gait training. **Days 21-28:** Final clearance, flying home with cane or unassisted. Most patients return to desk work in 4-6 weeks, full activities in 3 months.

4. What implant brands are used in Chengdu?

Top Chengdu hospitals use FDA/NMPA-approved implants from global leaders: **Zimmer Biomet (Persona, NexGen)** — US gold standard. **Stryker (Triathlon, Mako)** — robotic-compatible. **Smith & Nephew (Journey, Legion)** — UK. **DePuy Synthes (Attune, PFC)** — Johnson & Johnson. **AK Medical (Chinese, comparable quality at lower cost)**. Avoid no-name or off-brand implants. Verify implant serial number in your discharge summary.

5. Can I do both knees at the same time in Chengdu?

**Yes — bilateral TKR is common in Chengdu.** Advantages: one surgery, one recovery, 30-40% lower cost. Disadvantages: higher initial rehab intensity, slightly higher blood clot risk, longer hospital stay (7-10 nights vs 5-7). Best for: younger, healthier patients under 70, no significant heart/lung issues, motivated for intensive rehab. West China Hospital performs 500+ bilateral TKRs per year with excellent outcomes.
