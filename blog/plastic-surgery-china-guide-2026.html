5. Most Popular Procedures: Techniques & What to Expect

Double Eyelid Surgery (Asian Blepharoplasty)

Best For: Creating a visible upper eyelid crease for patients with monolids or heavy eyelids

Techniques:

  • Incisional Method: Permanent results, ideal for patients with excess skin or fat. Recovery: 2-3 weeks initial, 3-6 months final.
  • Suture Method: Less invasive, no incision. Results last 5-10 years. Recovery: 1 week.
  • Partial Incision: Hybrid approach for moderate correction. Recovery: 1-2 weeks.

Price Range: $1,500 - $3,000

Complication Rate: 1-3% at accredited facilities

Rhinoplasty (Nose Reshaping)

Best For: Changing nose shape, size, bridge height, tip refinement, or correcting breathing issues

Techniques:

  • Open Rhinoplasty: For major reshaping with visible columellar incision. Better visualization.
  • Closed Rhinoplasty: Incisions inside nostrils only. No visible scarring, faster recovery.
  • Tip Plasty: Focused on tip refinement only. Less invasive.
  • Revision Rhinoplasty: Correcting previous surgery results. More complex.

Price Range: $3,000 - $6,000 (Primary), $5,000 - $12,000 (Revision)

Recovery: 1-2 weeks for social activities, splint removal at 1 week, final shape at 6-12 months

Facelift (Rhytidectomy)

Best For: Sagging skin, jowls, deep nasolabial folds, loss of jawline definition

Techniques:

  • Traditional Facelift: Comprehensive correction from temple to neck. Addresses SMAS layer.
  • Mini Facelift: Less invasive, shorter scar, faster recovery. Ideal for early aging.
  • Thread Lift: Minimally invasive using dissolvable threads. Results last 1-3 years.
  • Neck Lift: Often combined with facelift for comprehensive lower face rejuvenation.

Price Range: $4,000 - $8,000 (Traditional), $2,500 - $5,000 (Mini)

Recovery: 2-3 weeks initial, drains typically removed within 3-5 days, final results at 3-6 months

Breast Augmentation

Best For: Increasing breast size, restoring volume after weight loss/pregnancy, improving symmetry

Implant Options:

  • Silicone Implants: Most popular, more natural feel. Sizes 200-600cc available.
  • Saline Implants: Adjustable during surgery, smaller incision possible.
  • Composite/Gummy Bear: Form-stable implants maintaining shape.

Incision Options: Inframammary (most common), periareolar, transaxillary

Placement: Submuscular (more coverage) vs. subglandular (faster recovery)

Price Range: $4,000 - $7,000 (includes implants)

Lifespan: 10-20 years; MRI recommended every 2-3 years for silicone

Liposuction & Body Contouring

Best For: Removing stubborn fat deposits resistant to diet and exercise

Techniques:

  • Tumescent Liposuction: Local anesthesia with saline solution. Gold standard.
  • Vaser Liposuction: Ultrasound-assisted for better skin retraction.
  • Laser Lipolysis: Laser-assisted for smaller areas, skin tightening benefits.
  • Hi-Def Liposuction: Advanced technique for muscle definition (ABS, arms).

Common Treatment Areas: Abdomen, flanks, thighs, arms, chin, back, male chest

Price Range: $1,500 - $3,000 per area; $6,000 - $15,000 for multiple areas

Recovery: Compression garment 4-6 weeks, return to work in 1-2 weeks, final results at 3-6 months

Botox & Dermal Fillers (Non-Surgical)

Botox: Neuromodulator for dynamic wrinkles (forehead, frown lines, crow's feet). Results last 3-4 months.

Fillers: Hyaluronic acid or other substances for volume restoration. Common areas: lips, cheeks, nasolabial folds, under eyes. Results last 6-18 months depending on product.

Price Range: Botox $200-400 per area; Fillers $400-800 per syringe

7. Understanding Risks & How to Minimize Them

Like all surgical procedures, plastic surgery carries inherent risks. Understanding these risks and choosing the right facility and surgeon significantly reduces complications.

🔴 General Surgical Risks

  • Infection: Risk is 1-2% at accredited facilities with proper sterile technique. Symptoms include fever, increasing redness, discharge.
  • Bleeding/Hematoma: Most common in first 24-48 hours. May require surgical drainage.
  • Adverse anesthesia reactions: Rare (<0.5%) with modern anesthetics and proper pre-operative assessment.
  • Blood clots (DVT/PE): Risk increases with prolonged immobility. Early mobilization and compression devices help prevent.
  • Poor wound healing: More common in smokers, diabetics, and those with poor nutrition.

🟠 Procedure-Specific Risks

  • Rhinoplasty: Breathing difficulty, asymmetry, over-reduction requiring revision, anosmia (temporary)
  • Breast Augmentation: Capsular contracture (hardening), implant rupture, change in nipple sensation, asymmetry
  • Liposuction: Contour irregularities, seroma (fluid collection), skin dimpling or laxity
  • Facelift: Facial nerve injury (rare, often temporary), hematoma, hair loss near incisions, ear lobe distortion
  • Double Eyelid: Asymmetry, incomplete crease formation, difficulty closing eyes (temporary)

How to Minimize Your Risks

  1. Choose JCI-accredited facilities: These meet international safety standards
  2. Verify surgeon credentials: Board certification, years of experience, before/after galleries
  3. Complete pre-operative testing: Blood work, EKG for patients over 40, imaging as needed
  4. Disclose complete medical history: Medications, supplements, previous surgeries, allergies
  5. Follow pre-operative instructions: Stop smoking (4-6 weeks before), avoid blood thinners
  6. Arrange proper aftercare: Stay near hospital for initial recovery period
  7. Budget for possible revision: Even with best surgeons, 5-10% may need adjustment

⚠️ Red Flags to Avoid

  • Extremely low prices that seem too good to be true
  • Surgeons who guarantee results or don't discuss risks
  • Facilities without proper accreditation or emergency equipment
  • Pressure tactics to book immediately
  • No before/after photos or unwillingness to provide references
  • Surgeons who are not board-certified in plastic surgery

8. Preparing for Your Plastic Surgery in China

4-6 Weeks Before Surgery

  • Stop smoking completely or at least 4 weeks before surgery
  • Discontinue blood-thinning medications (aspirin, ibuprofen, warfarin) after consulting your doctor
  • Avoid herbal supplements that may increase bleeding (ginkgo, garlic, ginseng, vitamin E)
  • Maintain stable weight; major weight fluctuations affect results
  • Complete any required medical tests locally and send results to your surgeon
  • Arrange travel insurance that covers medical complications

2 Weeks Before Surgery

  • Finalize travel arrangements; arrive at least 2-3 days before surgery
  • Book accommodation near the hospital for easy follow-up visits
  • Confirm all payments and understand cancellation policies
  • Prepare questions for your surgeon during final consultation
  • Arrange for a companion/escort for first 24-72 hours post-surgery
  • Stock up on soft foods, button-front clothing, and recovery supplies

The Day Before Surgery

  • Fast from midnight (no food or drink, except small sips of water for essential medications)
  • Get a good night's sleep; avoid alcohol
  • Prepare documents: passport, medical records, blood type card, emergency contacts
  • Remove all jewelry, nail polish, and contact lenses
  • Wear loose, comfortable clothing that buttons in the front

What to Bring

  • Compression garments (if recommended by your surgeon)
  • Prescription medications you regularly take
  • Comfortable pillow for travel
  • Entertainment (books, tablet) for recovery
  • Cash and credit cards for incidental expenses
  • Loose, button-down clothing for easy dressing post-surgery

9. Recovery Timeline & Aftercare

General Recovery Guidelines by Procedure

First 24-48 Hours:

  • Rest with head elevated (2-3 pillows) to minimize swelling
  • Take prescribed pain medications as directed; don't wait for pain to become severe
  • Apply ice packs as recommended (typically 20 minutes on, 20 minutes off)
  • Walk gently to promote circulation, but avoid strenuous activity
  • Stick to clear liquids and bland foods; nausea is common after anesthesia

Week 1:

  • Most patients return to desk work or light activities
  • Stitches typically removed at day 5-7 (depending on procedure)
  • Bruising and swelling peak, then begin to subside
  • Continue wearing compression garments if prescribed
  • Avoid bending over, heavy lifting, and strenuous activities

Weeks 2-4:

  • Gradually resume normal activities; light exercise OK for most procedures
  • Swelling continues to decrease significantly
  • Most patients feel comfortable going out in public
  • Incisions beginning to fade from red to pink
  • Follow surgeon's guidance on scar care products

Months 2-6:

  • 90% of swelling resolved
  • Results becoming apparent; final shape emerging
  • Resuming all normal activities including exercise
  • Scar maturation: fading from pink to pale
  • Follow-up appointments to assess progress

6-12 Months:

  • Final results visible
  • Scar maturation complete (silky, pale appearance)
  • Any revisions typically performed after 12 months if needed

When to Contact Your Surgeon Immediately

  • Fever above 101°F (38.5°C)
  • Sudden severe pain not relieved by medication
  • Excessive bleeding or discharge from incision
  • Signs of infection: increasing redness, warmth, swelling
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Signs of blood clot: severe leg swelling, chest pain, shortness of breath

10. Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to get plastic surgery in China?

Yes, when performed at accredited facilities by qualified surgeons. China has numerous JCI-accredited hospitals with excellent safety records. The key is thorough research: choose accredited hospitals, verify surgeon credentials, and follow all pre- and post-operative instructions. Medical tourism to China has grown significantly, with hospitals catering specifically to international patients.

How do I communicate with medical staff if I don't speak Chinese?

Major hospitals serving international patients have dedicated English-speaking staff and medical coordinators. Many have interpreters for other languages including Korean, Japanese, Russian, and Arabic. Some hospitals offer video consultations before travel where you can discuss everything in your native language.

How long do I need to stay in China after surgery?

The minimum recommended stay depends on the procedure:

  • Botox/Fillers: Same day or 1 day
  • Double Eyelid: 7-10 days
  • Rhinoplasty: 10-14 days
  • Breast Surgery: 10-14 days
  • Liposuction: 7-10 days
  • Facelift: 14-21 days

This allows for initial recovery, stitch removal, and follow-up consultations before traveling home.

Will I need someone to accompany me?

For major procedures under general anesthesia, yes. You will need a responsible adult to accompany you for at least the first 24-48 hours. If you don't have someone traveling with you, hospitals can arrange medical nurses or patient liaisons to assist during your recovery.

What happens if I need a revision?

Revision rates vary by procedure and surgeon experience (typically 5-10% for rhinoplasty, lower for other procedures). Discuss revision policies before booking:

  • Some surgeons include one revision in their fee within a certain timeframe
  • You may need to travel back to China for revisions
  • Budget for potential revision costs (usually significantly less than original surgery)
  • Get everything in writing regarding revision policies

How do I verify a surgeon's credentials?

Ask the hospital for the surgeon's:

  • Medical license number (verifiable through Chinese Medical Association)
  • Board certification documentation
  • Number of years in practice
  • Number of procedures performed annually
  • Photos of previous patients with similar concerns
  • Professional society memberships (ISAPS, Chinese Association of Plastic Surgery)

Can I combine multiple procedures?

Yes, combination surgery is common and can be cost-effective. Popular combinations include:

  • Mommy Makeover: Tummy tuck + Breast surgery + Liposuction
  • Facial rejuvenation: Facelift + Eyelid surgery + Brow lift
  • Body contouring: Liposuction + Fat transfer to buttocks or breasts

Your surgeon will advise on safe combinations based on procedure length and your health status. Combined procedures typically take 4-8 hours and require longer recovery.
