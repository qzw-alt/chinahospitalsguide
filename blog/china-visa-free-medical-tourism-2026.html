🎯 Key Visa-Free Options for Medical Tourists

✅ 144-Hour Transit Visa Exemption

CITIZENS OF 53 COUNTRIES can transit through major Chinese cities (including Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Hangzhou, Chengdu, Xiamen, Qingdao, etc.) WITHOUT any visa for up to 144 hours (6 days). Medical treatment qualifies as a valid reason for stay.

📋 Requirements for Visa-Free Medical Entry

Basic Requirements:

  • Valid passport (6+ months validity)
  • Confirmed return/onward ticket
  • Proof of accommodation in China
  • Travel itinerary showing entry/exit dates
  • Medical appointment confirmation from a Chinese hospital

For Extended Stay (Medical Treatment):

If your treatment requires more than 144 hours, you can:

  • Apply for a Medical Visa (M Visa) at Chinese embassies/consulates before travel
  • Use Hainan's special medical visa zone (允许多次入境)
  • Partner with a medical coordinator who can assist with visa extension

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I really enter China without a visa for medical treatment?

A: Yes! If you're from one of the 53 eligible countries and can provide proof of onward travel, you can enter China visa-free for up to 144 hours. Medical tourism is an accepted purpose of stay.

Q: What if my treatment requires more than 6 days?

A: We recommend applying for an M (Medical) Visa before your trip. Our coordinators can help prepare all required documentation, including hospital invitation letters.

Q: Is Hainan really open for medical tourism visa-free?

A: Hainan Province has expanded visa-free access specifically for medical tourists. Foreign patients can enter Hainan through designated ports with health screening and receive treatment at accredited medical institutions.

Q: Can I use health insurance?
