Endometriosis Surgery in China 2026: Costs, Top Hospitals & What to Expect

Written by China Hospitals Guide · Updated May 2026 · 2,400+ words

Endometriosis affects roughly 1 in 10 women of reproductive age worldwide, causing chronic pelvic pain, heavy periods, and infertility. When medication fails to control symptoms, surgery becomes the most effective option. For international patients, China has emerged as a leading destination: world-class endometriosis surgery at a fraction of Western prices, performed by high-volume surgeons at JCI-accredited hospitals.

If you are considering endometriosis surgery abroad, this guide covers everything you need to know about procedure types, costs, top hospitals, and how to plan your medical trip to China in 2026.

Key Facts at a Glance

  • Surgery cost in China: $3,000-12,000 (vs. $15,000-30,000 in the US)
  • Top procedure: Laparoscopic excision (gold standard for complete removal)
  • Hospital stay: 3-5 days for laparoscopic surgery
  • Recommended stay in China: 10-14 days total
  • JCI-accredited hospitals: Available in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Hangzhou

What Is Endometriosis Surgery?

Endometriosis surgery involves removing endometrial-like tissue that has grown outside the uterus, most commonly on the ovaries, fallopian tubes, bladder, bowel, and pelvic lining. The goal is to relieve pain, reduce symptoms, and — for patients seeking fertility — improve reproductive outcomes.

Surgery is typically recommended when:

  • Pain is severe and not controlled by medication (NSAIDs, hormonal therapy)
  • Endometriomas (ovarian cysts) larger than 4 cm are present
  • Infertility is linked to anatomical distortion from endometriosis
  • Endometrial tissue is pressing on nerves, bladder, or bowel

Types of Endometriosis Surgery Available in China

Chinese hospitals offer the full spectrum of endometriosis procedures, matching or exceeding what's available in Western medical centers. The right approach depends on the severity of your condition, your symptom profile, and whether you plan future pregnancies.

Laparoscopic Excision Surgery

This is the gold standard for endometriosis removal. Surgeons make 3-4 small incisions (5-12 mm each) and insert a camera and specialized instruments to excise endometrial implants while preserving healthy tissue. For patients with moderate to severe endometriosis, laparoscopic excision offers the best long-term pain relief and highest pregnancy rates for those trying to conceive naturally or with IVF.

At high-volume Chinese centers, surgeons perform 200+ laparoscopic endometriosis procedures per year, which far exceeds the caseload of most Western gynecological surgeons.

Robotic-Assisted da Vinci Surgery

For deeply infiltrating endometriosis (DIE) involving the rectum, bladder, or ureters, robotic-assisted surgery offers greater precision than conventional laparoscopy. The da Vinci Surgical System provides 3D visualization and wristed instruments that move with greater dexterity inside the pelvis.

China has one of the largest da Vinci robotic surgery programs globally, with over 300 installed systems nationwide. Centers in Beijing and Shanghai routinely perform complex endometriosis resections robotically.

Hysteroscopic Surgery

When endometriosis involves the inside of the uterus (adenomyosis), hysteroscopic surgery — using a thin camera passed through the cervix without any incision — can remove focal lesions and reduce heavy bleeding. This is often combined with laparoscopic surgery for comprehensive treatment.

Radical Surgery (Hysterectomy with Salpingo-Oophorectomy)

In cases of severe, recurrent endometriosis where fertility preservation is not a goal, radical surgery removing the uterus, fallopian tubes, and ovaries may be considered. This is a last resort and requires thorough pre-surgical counseling. Chinese surgeons are conservative with this approach and typically exhaust all fertility-sparing options first.

Top Hospitals for Endometriosis Surgery in China

Not all Chinese hospitals have equal expertise in endometriosis. The hospitals listed below are recognized nationally for their gynecological surgery programs, high-volume case experience, and international patient services.

Peking Union Medical College Hospital (Beijing)

PUMCH is consistently ranked the top hospital in China for complex medical cases. Its Department of Obstetrics and Gynecology is a national referral center for severe endometriosis, including cases involving bowel, bladder, and ureteral involvement. Surgeons here routinely handle the most complex cases rejected by other institutions. The hospital has a dedicated International Medical Center with English-speaking coordinators.

Shanghai First Maternity and Infant Hospital (Shanghai)

One of China's busiest maternity and gynecology hospitals, with a specialized endometriosis center that handles both fertility-focused and pain-management surgical cases. The hospital's laparoscopic surgery program is particularly strong, with surgeons performing more than 500 gynecological laparoscopies annually. English-language medical documentation is standardized.

Guangzhou Women and Children's Medical Center (Guangzhou)

A leading women's and children's hospital in southern China with a high-volume endometriosis treatment program. The hospital is affiliated with Guangzhou Medical University and offers both conventional and robotic-assisted surgical approaches. It has served patients from Southeast Asia, the Middle East, and Europe.

Sir Run Run Shaw Hospital, Zhejiang University (Hangzhou)

Part of the Zhejiang University healthcare network, this hospital has a strong minimally invasive surgery program and is JCI-accredited. Its gynecology department specializes in laparoscopic endometriosis excision and has published clinical outcomes data for review.

How to Prepare for Endometriosis Surgery in China

Proper preparation can significantly reduce anxiety and improve surgical outcomes. Here is what international patients should arrange before traveling to China for endometriosis surgery.

Medical Records and Diagnostics

Before your trip, send the following to your chosen hospital for review:

  • MRI or ultrasound imaging confirming endometriosis diagnosis (films + written report)
  • Laparoscopy or hysteroscopy reports from any previous procedures
  • Current medications list (especially hormonal therapies, blood thinners)
  • CA-125 blood test results if available
  • Fertility test results (AMH, antral follicle count) if applicable

Top hospitals will review these records via email before your arrival and provide a preliminary treatment plan and cost estimate.

Visa and Travel Arrangements

International patients need a medical visa (M visa) to travel to China for treatment. Your hospital or medical tourism coordinator can provide a visa invitation letter (Letter of Notice for Foreign Patients) required for the M visa application. Processing time is typically 4-8 business days.

If you are from one of the 15 countries with China's 144-hour transit visa exemption, you may use that for an initial consultation, but full surgery requires a formal M visa.

Accommodation and Recovery

Most international patients stay in hotels near the hospital for the pre-operative consultation, hospital admission, and initial post-discharge recovery. Many JCI-accredited hospitals have affiliated or partner hotels with discounted rates for medical tourists. Plan for a minimum 10-14 day stay in China:

  • Days 1-2: Arrival, consultation, pre-operative testing
  • Days 3-7: Hospital admission and surgery recovery
  • Days 8-14: Post-discharge follow-up appointments and recovery before air travel

What to Expect During Recovery

Recovery from laparoscopic endometriosis surgery is generally faster than open abdominal surgery, but it still requires careful post-operative management.

Immediate Post-Op (First Week)

Most patients spend 3-5 days in the hospital after laparoscopic endometriosis surgery. Pain is managed with IV and oral medications. You will be encouraged to walk within 24 hours of surgery to reduce the risk of blood clots. A clear liquid diet is typically started the day after surgery, advancing as bowel function returns.

Common symptoms in the first week include:

  • Shoulder pain from residual CO2 gas used during laparoscopy (usually resolves in 2-3 days)
  • Mild bloating and abdominal tenderness
  • Fatigue, which can last several weeks
  • Vaginal bleeding or discharge for up to 2 weeks

Weeks 2-4 After Surgery

By the second week, most patients feel well enough to travel home, provided the surgeon clears them for air travel. Full return to work or normal activities typically takes 4-6 weeks for laparoscopic surgery and 6-8 weeks for robotic or open surgery.

Your surgeon will provide:

  • Complete medical report in English, including operative findings and pathology results
  • Post-operative medication prescriptions (available internationally)
  • Follow-up schedule for remote consultations via video call or email
  • Recommendations for hormonal therapy to prevent recurrence

Long-Term Outcomes

Studies from high-volume endometriosis centers in China report pain relief in 80-90% of patients after laparoscopic excision surgery. For patients with infertility, meta-analyses show pregnancy rates of 40-60% within 12 months of surgery, with higher rates when IVF is combined with surgical treatment.

Endometriosis Surgery and Fertility: What You Need to Know

Endometriosis is a leading cause of infertility, affecting egg quality, fallopian tube function, and embryo implantation. For patients who want to preserve or improve fertility, surgical treatment must be carefully planned.

In China, fertility-sparing endometriosis surgery is the standard approach for women of reproductive age. Surgeons use techniques that minimize trauma to the ovaries (particularly important when endometriomas are present) and preserve normal pelvic anatomy. For patients planning IVF, many Chinese fertility centers offer integrated surgical + IVF treatment packages.

If you are planning a pregnancy after surgery, discuss the optimal timing with your surgeon. Generally, waiting 3-6 months after surgery before attempting conception is recommended to allow inflammation to resolve and hormonal therapy (if used) to be completed.

Why Choose China for Endometriosis Surgery?

China has become a serious contender for international patients seeking endometriosis surgery for several reasons that go beyond price alone.

High-Volume Surgeon Experience

Chinese gynecological surgeons at major referral centers handle far more endometriosis cases than their Western counterparts. A surgeon at PUMCH may perform 300+ endometriosis procedures per year, compared to a typical US gynecologist's 20-50 cases annually. High volume correlates with better outcomes and lower complication rates in complex pelvic surgery.

Advanced Surgical Technology

China's investment in medical infrastructure means top hospitals have the latest da Vinci robotic systems, 3D laparoscopy towers, and dedicated endometriosis surgery equipment. Many Chinese centers were early adopters of enhanced recovery protocols (ERAS) that reduce post-operative hospital stays without compromising safety.

Integrated Traditional Chinese Medicine

Some hospitals offer optional post-operative TCM support — including herbal formulations and acupuncture — to help manage post-surgical inflammation and support recovery. This is offered as a complement, not replacement, to conventional post-operative care.

No Long Wait Times

In countries with public healthcare systems, endometriosis surgery wait times can exceed 12-18 months. In China, you can typically schedule surgery within 2-4 weeks of your initial consultation, even at top-tier JCI hospitals.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does endometriosis surgery cost in China?

Laparoscopic endometriosis excision in China costs $3,500-8,000, while robotic-assisted surgery ranges $8,000-12,000. This compares to $15,000-30,000 in the United States and GBP 9,000-14,000 for private treatment in the UK. The cost savings of 65-75% make China one of the most cost-effective destinations for high-quality endometriosis surgery.

What are the best hospitals for endometriosis surgery in China?

The top hospitals for endometriosis surgery in China include Peking Union Medical College Hospital in Beijing (best for complex and recurrent cases), Shanghai First Maternity and Infant Hospital (high-volume laparoscopic center), Guangzhou Women and Children's Medical Center (southern China hub), and Sir Run Run Shaw Hospital in Hangzhou (JCI-accredited minimally invasive surgery). All have dedicated international patient departments with English-language support.

Is endometriosis surgery in China safe for international patients?

Yes. JCI-accredited Chinese hospitals meet international safety and quality standards. Top centers have dedicated international patient departments with English-speaking coordinators, medical translators for other languages, and experience treating patients from 100+ countries. All surgeons at these hospitals are board-certified specialists with additional training in complex pelvic surgery.

What types of endometriosis surgery are available in China?

China offers the full range of endometriosis procedures: laparoscopic excision (the gold standard), robotic-assisted da Vinci surgery for deeply infiltrating disease, hysteroscopic surgery for intrauterine involvement, and combined approaches for severe multi-organ cases. Surgeons at top centers are experienced in both fertility-sparing conservative surgery and radical procedures when indicated.

How long do I need to stay in China after endometriosis surgery?

Most international patients need to stay in China for 10-14 days after endometriosis surgery. This includes the hospital stay (3-5 days for laparoscopic surgery) and post-discharge recovery before air travel. Your surgeon will assess your readiness for return travel based on your specific procedure, recovery progress, and any post-operative complications. Flying immediately after surgery is not recommended due to the risk of blood clots.

Will I need hormonal therapy after endometriosis surgery?

Post-operative hormonal therapy (such as GnRH agonists or combined oral contraceptives) is commonly recommended to suppress any remaining microscopic endometrial tissue and reduce the risk of recurrence. Your surgeon will discuss the best option based on your symptoms, surgical findings, and whether you are planning a future pregnancy.
