At that price, a full upper and lower restoration — the kind that would run $50,000-$100,000 in the US — comes in at roughly $16,000-$30,000 in China. This guide covers costs, top hospitals, treatment timelines, candidacy, and what foreign patients actually experience.

If you are missing most or all of your teeth, or your remaining teeth are failing, you have probably already discovered how expensive full-mouth reconstruction is in the United States, United Kingdom, or Australia. The price quotes are staggering — $40,000 to $100,000 for a full set of implants. What many patients do not realize is that China has become a serious option for this procedure, with dental implant systems, experienced oral surgeons, and JCI-accredited hospitals that cater specifically to international patients.

This article focuses specifically on the All-on-4 dental implant system — one of the most well-established full-arch solutions available — and what it costs and involves when done in China in 2026.

What Is All-on-4 — and Why Does It Matter?

All-on-4 is a surgical technique developed by Nobel Biocare in the late 1990s that uses four dental implants to support a full fixed arch of replacement teeth. Unlike traditional implant approaches that require 8, 10, or more implants per arch, All-on-4 minimizes surgical complexity while providing a permanent, non-removable result.

The four implants are placed at precise angles — typically two vertical in the front and two angled up to 45 degrees in the back — to maximize contact with available bone. This often eliminates the need for bone grafting, which is one of the most time-consuming and expensive preparatory procedures in traditional implantology.

Within 24-72 hours of implant placement, a temporary fixed bridge is attached. Patients leave the hospital with functional teeth. After 3-6 months of healing, the final permanent prosthetic is fabricated and installed.

The All-on-4 Treatment Timeline

All-on-4 treatment in China typically unfolds across two main visits. Here is what the timeline looks like:

Visit 1: Initial Assessment and Temporary Bridge (Days 1-10)

Most international patients schedule a 7-10 day initial trip. Here is what happens during those days:

  • Day 1: Arrival, hotel check-in, initial hospital consultation, CBCT scan and 3D imaging
  • Day 2: Comprehensive oral examination, treatment plan finalization, pre-operative lab work
  • Day 3-4: All-on-4 surgery (2-4 hours per arch under local anesthesia with IV sedation)
  • Day 5-6: Recovery, follow-up appointment, impressions taken for temporary bridge
  • Day 7-10: Temporary fixed acrylic bridge fitted and adjusted, final instructions provided, patient departs

Healing Period (3-6 Months)

The implants fuse with the jawbone through osseointegration. During this period, patients eat a modified diet (soft foods only) and attend any needed follow-ups with their local dentist. The temporary bridge remains in place throughout healing.

Visit 2: Final Prosthetic (3-5 Days)

Once healing is complete, patients return to China for 3-5 days to have the permanent zirconia or PFM bridge installed. The old temporary bridge is removed, impressions are taken for the final prosthetic, and the permanent bridge is fabricated and fitted over 2-3 days.

Who Is a Candidate for All-on-4?

All-on-4 is designed for patients who are missing most or all teeth in one or both arches, or whose remaining teeth are severely compromised. It is particularly well-suited for:

  • Patients missing all teeth in an arch who want a fixed (non-removable) solution
  • Patients with failing teeth who need full-arch extraction and immediate replacement
  • Patients who currently wear removable dentures and want a permanent alternative
  • Patients with moderate bone loss who want to avoid bone grafting procedures

The ideal candidate has sufficient jawbone density to support four implants, is in reasonable general health for surgery, and does not have uncontrolled systemic conditions such as severe diabetes or active smoking (smoking significantly increases implant failure risk).

Patients with severe bone loss may require All-on-6 instead (six implants for greater support), or bone grafting followed by delayed implant placement. A good hospital will assess this via CBCT scan on Day 1 and give you a honest recommendation — not upsell you to a more expensive procedure you do not need.

Age Considerations

There is no upper age limit for All-on-4 provided the patient is healthy enough for surgery. Younger patients (under 60) tend to heal faster and have better long-term osseointegration outcomes. However, many patients in their 60s, 70s, and even 80s have successfully undergone All-on-4 treatment in China with appropriate medical clearance.

What Is Included in an All-on-4 Quote — and What Is Not

Before committing, ask the hospital for a written itemized quote. Here is a checklist of what should be included:

  • Surgical fee (implant placement, 4 implants)
  • Sedation or anesthesia fee
  • Temporary acrylic bridge (fitted within 72 hours of surgery)
  • Permanent prosthetic bridge (zirconia or PFM)
  • CBCT scan and digital treatment planning
  • All follow-up visits during the hospital stay
  • Medications prescribed during treatment
  • Airport or hotel transfer assistance

What may not be included:

  • Blood tests or general health clearance (usually separate, $50-$150)
  • Extractions of remaining failing teeth (often $100-$300 per tooth)
  • Bone grafting if needed (typically $500-$2,000 depending on extent)
  • Second trip flights and accommodation
  • Lifetime maintenance or warranty repairs at a local dentist

Pro Tip: Ask the hospital whether the quote is a "package price" or an estimate. A true package price locks in the total cost regardless of how many follow-up visits you need during your stay. Estimates can creep upward. Get everything in writing before you travel.

Risks and How to Minimize Them

No surgical procedure is risk-free. All-on-4 carries the same general risks as any dental implant surgery: infection, nerve injury, implant failure, and prosthetic complications. Here is how to minimize each:

  • Infection: Choose a JCI hospital with a dedicated infection control protocol. Follow post-operative care instructions carefully. Do not skip antibiotics if prescribed.
  • Implant failure: Success rates for All-on-4 implants in healthy patients are 95-98% at 10 years, according to Nobel Biocare's long-term clinical data. Failure risk is higher in smokers, uncontrolled diabetics, and patients with poor oral hygiene.
  • Nerve injury: The inferior alveolar nerve runs through the lower jaw. Angled implant placement in All-on-4 is specifically designed to avoid this nerve. CBCT-guided surgery reduces this risk further.
  • Prosthetic complications: The permanent bridge may chip or need adjustment over time. Ask the hospital about their warranty policy for the prosthetic — reputable hospitals typically offer 1-5 years of warranty.

What to Do If Your Local Dentist Does Not Support Chinese Implants

One of the most common concerns patients raise is whether their dentist back home can service implants placed in China. The answer is almost always yes — with one important caveat:

If your hospital uses a major global brand (Straumann, Nobel Biocare, Osstem, Astra Tech), any dentist anywhere in the world can work with those implant systems. When you complete treatment, ask your Chinese hospital for a treatment summary that includes the implant brand, model, and diameter — your local dentist will use this to identify the correct prosthetic components.

The only situation where local follow-up becomes complicated is if your hospital used a Chinese domestic implant brand that is not available outside China. Always confirm which brand is being used and verify your local dentist's ability to work with it before committing to treatment.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do All-on-4 dental implants cost in China in 2026?

All-on-4 dental implants in China cost $8,000-$15,000 per arch in 2026, compared to $24,000-$50,000 per arch in the United States. Most Chinese hospitals charge $1,500-$3,000 per implant for the prosthetic bridge, plus $5,000-$12,000 for the surgical placement of four implants. A full two-arch restoration in China costs $16,000-$30,000 versus $50,000-$100,000 in the US.

Is All-on-4 dental treatment safe in China?

Yes, All-on-4 treatment is safe in China when performed at a JCI-accredited hospital with experienced oral-maxillofacial surgeons. China has performed dental implant procedures since the 1980s, and many top hospitals use the same implant systems (Straumann, Nobel Biocare) available in Western countries. The key is choosing the right hospital — look for JCI accreditation, English-speaking staff, and a dedicated international patient department.

How long does All-on-4 treatment take in China?

The surgical phase takes 2-4 hours per arch under local anesthesia or IV sedation. The temporary fixed bridge is typically fitted within 48-72 hours after surgery. The final prosthetic bridge is installed after 3-6 months of healing, once osseointegration is complete. Most international patients plan a 7-10 day initial visit followed by a second short visit 3-6 months later for the permanent prosthetic.

What is the difference between All-on-4 and traditional dental implants?

Traditional full-mouth implants require 8-10 implants per arch, each placed individually with months of healing between procedures. All-on-4 uses just four strategically placed implants per arch to support a full fixed bridge, reducing surgical complexity, cost, and healing time. All-on-4 also avoids the need for bone grafting in most cases, even when some bone loss has occurred.

What implant brands are used in Chinese hospitals for All-on-4?

Most major Chinese hospitals offering All-on-4 use globally recognized implant brands including Straumann (Switzerland), Nobel Biocare (Switzerland), Osstem (South Korea), and Astra Tech (Sweden). All-on-4 was originally developed by Nobel Biocare, and their implant systems are widely used in China alongside Straumann's parallel-wall and tapered implants.

Can I get All-on-4 if I have existing bone loss?

Possibly. All-on-4 is specifically designed to work with less bone than traditional implants because the posterior implants are angled to maximize bone contact. However, severe bone loss may require bone grafting first or a different approach such as All-on-6 or zygomatic implants. A CBCT scan on arrival at the hospital will give your surgeon a clear picture of your bone situation and the most appropriate solution.
