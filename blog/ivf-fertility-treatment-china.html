4. Types of IVF Treatment Available in China

1. Standard IVF

Traditional in vitro fertilization where eggs and sperm are combined in a laboratory dish. Suitable for couples with unexplained infertility, tubal factor, or mild male factor.

2. ICSI (Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection)

Single sperm injected directly into egg. Recommended for male factor infertility, low sperm count, previous IVF failures, or unexplained infertility. Adds $1,500-$3,000 to standard IVF cost.

3. PGD/PGS (Preimplantation Genetic Testing)

Genetic testing of embryos before transfer to screen for chromosomal abnormalities (PGS) or specific genetic conditions (PGD). Particularly valuable for:

  • Couples with inherited genetic conditions
  • Advanced maternal age (35+)
  • Recurrent miscarriage history
  • Previous IVF failures

4. Egg Donation

Using donor eggs from screened, healthy young women (18-25 years). High success rates (60-70%) for women with diminished ovarian reserve, premature ovarian failure, or genetic concerns. Anonymous donors in China.

5. Embryo Donation

Using donated embryos from other couples who completed treatment. Most affordable option with good success rates. Embryos are thoroughly screened per Chinese regulations.

6. Frozen Embryo Transfer (FET)

Using previously frozen embryos. More affordable (~$1,500-$2,500) and allows for natural cycle timing. Vitrification techniques achieve 95%+ embryo survival rates.

7. Time-Lapse Embryo Monitoring

Advanced incubation with continuous imaging. Allows embryologists to select the best embryo without disturbing the culture environment. Available at premium centers.

8. China's Legal Framework for IVF

⚖️ Important Legal Information

Surrogacy is NOT legal in China. Commercial surrogacy arrangements are prohibited under Chinese law. Couples seeking surrogacy must pursue this in other countries.

What IS Legal in China:

  • IVF with own eggs/sperm — Fully legal for married heterosexual couples
  • IVF with donor eggs — Legal; donations are anonymous
  • IVF with donor sperm — Legal; sperm banks operate under strict regulations
  • Frozen embryo storage — Legal; typical storage limit is 10 years
  • PGD/PGS — Legal for medical indications (genetic disease prevention)
  • Single embryo transfer (SET) — Encouraged by Chinese Medical Association guidelines to reduce multiple pregnancy risks

Requirements for IVF in China:

  • Marriage certificate — Most hospitals require proof of marriage (heterosexual couples)
  • Medical indication — Documented fertility diagnosis
  • Valid ID — Passport for international patients
  • Medical visa (M visa) — Required; hospitals provide invitation letters
  • Age limit — Typically 45-50 years for women (varies by hospital)

Single Embryo Transfer Policy

China's health authorities actively promote single embryo transfer to reduce twin/triplet rates and associated health risks. This policy:

  • Improves maternal safety
  • Reduces premature birth risk
  • Is consistent with international best practices
  • May require multiple cycles for same success probability

9. IVF Risks & Complications

⚠️ Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome (OHSS)

Risk Level: 1-5% (moderate to severe)

Occurs when ovaries respond too strongly to fertility drugs. Symptoms include abdominal bloating, nausea, and in severe cases, fluid accumulation. Modern protocols with GnRH antagonists have reduced OHSS risk significantly. China clinics monitor closely and may freeze all embryos if OHSS risk is high.

⚠️ Multiple Pregnancy

Risk Level: Reduced by SET policy (currently ~15% twin rate in China)

While some clinics offer double embryo transfer, China's guidelines favor single embryo transfer. Twins/triplets carry higher risks: premature birth, low birth weight, preeclampsia, and gestational diabetes.

⚠️ Ectopic Pregnancy

Risk Level: 1-2% (slightly higher with IVF than natural conception)

Embryo implants outside the uterus. Requires immediate medical attention. China clinics perform early ultrasound to confirm intrauterine pregnancy location.

⚠️ Miscarriage

Risk Level: 15-25% (varies by age)

Similar to natural conception rates. Increases with maternal age. PGD/PGS reduces miscarriage risk by selecting genetically normal embryos.

⚠️ Egg Retrieval Procedure Risks

Risk Level: Very low (<1%)

Transvaginal egg retrieval is minimally invasive. Rare complications include bleeding, infection, or damage to nearby structures. Performed under sedation with ultrasound guidance.

Long-Term Safety Data

Studies tracking children conceived via IVF show:

  • No increased risk of birth defects compared to natural conception (when controlling for multiple pregnancy)
  • No increased cancer risk in mothers or children
  • Slightly higher singleton pre-term delivery — managed with careful monitoring

FAQs

How long do I need to stay in China for IVF?

Plan for 3-4 weeks in China for one complete IVF cycle. This includes egg retrieval and embryo transfer. Frozen embryo transfers may only require 1-2 weeks.

Is IVF in China safe?

Yes. Top Chinese fertility centers are internationally accredited (JCI, CAP) and follow strict protocols. Success rates are comparable to—and sometimes exceed—Western clinics. Embryology labs maintain ISO standards.

Can international patients do IVF in China?

Yes. Many hospitals have dedicated international patient departments with English-speaking staff. We assist with visa letters, airport transfers, accommodation, and interpretation.

Can I do multiple IVF cycles in China?

Absolutely. Many patients do 2-3 cycles. Costs are so affordable that many couples attempt 2-3 cycles for the price of one in the US. Most clinics offer package pricing for multiple cycles.

What's included in the IVF cost?

Typically includes: doctor consultations, ultrasound monitoring, egg retrieval, embryo culture, embryo transfer, and 1 year of embryo storage. Medications ($1,500-$3,000) are usually separate.

What about surrogacy in China?

Surrogacy is not legal in China. If you require surrogacy, you will need to pursue treatment in another country such as the USA, Canada, or Georgia.

Do I need a visa for IVF treatment in China?

Yes. We'll provide a medical visa invitation letter (M Visa) to support your application. Processing time is typically 5-7 business days.

What is the age limit for IVF in China?

Most hospitals have an upper age limit of 45-50 for women using own eggs. Donor egg programs may extend to age 50-55. Individual evaluations determine eligibility.
